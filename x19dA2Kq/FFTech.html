The Honest Truth

A fully uncompromised media rights tech stack (Attio Pro, MOLTEN, Vitrina AI) costs roughly ~$2,000/month.

To hit the $500 cap while serving 10 people, we must deploy surgical trade-offs. The question isn't "what is the sexiest SaaS stack?", but rather "what yields the highest deal velocity and catalog protection right now?"

Strategic Advantage: We are deploying a nimble commercial operating system. We are intentionally avoiding the bloated, 6-month deployment cycles of legacy media-rights platforms. Furthermore, operational revenue trackers in this stack do not replace your general accounting ledger (e.g., Xero) for tax and invoicing compliance.

Architectural Options

Three distinct paths translated into plain English, showing exactly what each tool does for the business.

Option 1: The Decoy

The "All-In-One" Illusion

Attempts to minimize tool sprawl by forcing the catalog, CRM, and task management into a single software platform.

$400/mo

Leaves $100 Headroom

SmartSuite

The "Everything" Binder

Tries to be your CRM, rights catalog, and project tracker all at once.

Cost: $150/mo (10 seats)

ChatGPT Business

The Drafter

Provides AI accounts for all 10 staff to help write emails and summarize notes.

Cost: $250/mo (10 seats)

Why we reject this:

  • Zero Lead Generation: Option 1 completely fails Requirement #4. Your sales team will have to manually hunt for leads on Google.
  • The Catalog Data Cap: The SmartSuite "binder" literally runs out of pages. It has a hard limit of 5,000 records. A 1,700-title catalog tracked across multiple territories will breach this in months, forcing a massive, unbudgeted software upgrade.
Recommended Architecture

The Commercial & Rights Backbone

A purpose-built hybrid stack. We use specific tools for the exact jobs they are best at. Protect the catalog, accelerate sales, and meet all 6 requirements safely.

$339/mo

Leaves $161 Headroom

Airtable

The Vault

The master ledger. It holds your 1,700-title catalog, territory rules, and pricing. It ensures you never accidentally double-sell exclusive rights.

Cost: $60/mo (3 Editors; remaining 7 staff are free Viewers)

HubSpot Starter

The Front Desk

The sales hub. This is where reps log client emails, track conversations, and drag deals visually from "Lead" to "Term Sheet".

Cost: $60/mo (4 Sales Seats)

ClickUp Business

The Factory Floor

The delivery hub. Once a deal is signed in HubSpot, it moves here so Ops knows exactly what files to deliver, and Finance tracks the revenue forecast.

Cost: $120/mo (10 Users)

Apollo.io

The Radar

The outbound scout. Actively scans B2B databases to find newly launched streaming platforms and extracts the emails of their content buyers.

Cost: $49/mo (Basic Lead Gen)

ChatGPT Business

The AI Drafter

The secure AI brain. Used by leadership to rapidly draft bilingual term sheets and summarize complex deal notes.

Cost: $50/mo (2 Power Users - templates shared with team)

Commercial ROI

  • Zero Double-Selling: Keeping the catalog locked in Airtable ensures data integrity across exclusive windows.
  • Accelerated Deal Velocity: Sales engages freely in HubSpot without breaking backend rights logic.
  • Strict Pipeline Hygiene: Automated handoffs to ClickUp prevent dropped delivery tasks or lost invoices.
Option 3: The Risk

The Heavy Automation Hub

Attempts to automate everything by wiring together complex API platforms.

$455/mo

Airtable (Full)

The Vault + Front Desk

Forces all 10 staff to use the database as their daily CRM and project tracker.

Cost: $200/mo (10 Paid Editors)

Instantly & Make.com

The Megaphone & Wiring

Sends thousands of cold emails and relies on custom code to stitch data together.

Cost: ~$205/mo (w/ ChatGPT & Portals)

Why we reject this:

  • Execution Failure: Interconnecting 4 platforms via Make.com APIs inside a 90-day window is a severe operational risk. It breaks easily.
  • UI Friction: Airtable is a phenomenal database, but a clunky CRM. Forcing sales teams to use it daily kills adoption.
  • Wrong Market Fit: Instantly's mass-email approach ignores the reality that Chinese B2B partners communicate almost exclusively via WeChat.

*Prices based on verified standard annual billing logic (ClickUp $12/u, HubSpot Starter ~$15/u, Airtable $20/editor). Taxes excluded.
