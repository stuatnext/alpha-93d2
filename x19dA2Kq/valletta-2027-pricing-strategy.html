SiGMA is a directional Malta iGaming benchmark, not a like-for-like comparator — it runs 12k–30k delegates vs our ~5–6k.

Pricing non-negotiables

  • No single discount >10% without director approval; cumulative discounts (bundle + early-bird + multi-year) capped at 15%.
  • No discount below floor without director approval.
  • No custom activation sold without Ops approval.
  • Rebook price never applies to expanded scope.
  • No panel sold without content approval.
  • No "Misc" line items; no €0 sponsor assets unless part of a contracted package.
  • Commission paid on net realised revenue (after discount), not gross — rewards discount discipline; team bonus for holding ≥92% realisation.

Pricing

How the 2027 prices are set

Every 2027 increase is justified on four levers (shown per product in the Notes column): 1. Demand — 2026 sell-through & realised price; 2. Benchmark — gap to SiGMA (Malta) and our NYC internal rates; 3. Scarcity / exclusivity — how rare and ownable the asset is; 4. Value — deliverables attached. Volume anchors (e.g. the 3x3 stand) are deliberately restrained to protect the volume the model relies on; aggression is concentrated on scarce and experiential inventory.

New deliverable ideas — justifying the 2027 step-up (for discussion)

Price has risen; visible value must rise with it, or buyers ask "what more do I get?" Ideas below, mapped roughly to tiers — all need ops/content capacity to deliver, so treat as a menu to discuss, not commitments.

Headline / Diamond
Curated executive introductions (ABM matchmaking) · dedicated account lead / concierge · pre-event strategy session + post-event ROI review · priority placement & first right of refusal for next year.
Year-round media (NEXT.io engine)
Off-season visibility: editorial feature, podcast guest slot, newsletter inclusion, social campaign — turns a 2-day event into a 12-month partnership.
Data & leads
Badge / lead scanning, facilitated qualified meetings, post-event attendee insight reports — moves the pitch from "logo" to "pipeline".
Content amplification
Onsite filmed interview, full session video package, professional content cut for the sponsor's own channels.
Operator access
Hosted operator introductions / curated dinners tied to spend — leaning into Valletta's 40% operator density, the thing suppliers actually pay for.
Status & experience
VIP hospitality upgrades, speaker-faculty access, premium onsite status — scarce, high-margin, reinforces the tier ladder.
Post-event ROI report (standard, Platinum+)
Badge scans, attendee titles/companies (opt-in), and a pipeline-value estimate — a standard inclusion for Platinum & Diamond, not an upsell. Low cost, high perceived value; directly protects renewals.

Revenue

Rebook strategy — containing the leak without reneging

Most sponsors already know their 2026 price; reneging isn't an option and would damage trust. The leak is contained not by hiking the rebook price, but by narrowing what the rebook price covers and channelling all growth to 2027 pricing.

  • Same product, same scope = 2026 price. The legacy loyalty rate — honoured, no surprises.
  • Rebook = the 2026 catalogue rate, not a one-off 2026 discount. Any discount last year was a one-time incentive, not a permanent floor — one consistent answer for everyone.
  • Everything else is 2027 pricing: added products, larger booths, exclusivity upgrades, custom activations, and any renamed / materially expanded product.
  • Frame 2026 as a loyalty rate against the higher 2027 public anchor — the rebook now visibly feels like a win, which makes the upsell easier rather than defensive.
  • 2027 rebook-discount deadline — mid-August 2026: existing sponsors keep their 2026 rate for identical scope only if they rebook by mid-August 2026; after that, renewals move to 2027 pricing. Growth (upgrades, Diamond, new products) is at 2027 pricing regardless. Communicate the deadline to all 2026 partners in writing now.
  • Set the rebook discipline for 2028: open 2028 rebooking onsite at the 2027 event with an early-bird window (e.g. signed within 30 days), locking revenue ahead and building genuine renewal urgency for future cycles.
  • Spend-based ticket entitlement applies to rebookers too — a soft value rebalancing (perks align to the new model) without touching the headline price.
  • Upsell into Diamond / bundles / new products at 2027 prices — the rebooked core stays flat, the growth is all new-money at the new rate.

Net effect: leakage is limited to identical-scope renewals only; every expansion, upgrade and new buyer is captured at 2027 pricing.
