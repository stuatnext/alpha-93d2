Briefing for Monday

Commercial RevOps Team, Scope and Role Clarity

This briefing explains the intended strategy, structure and role scope. The aim is to align on wording without weakening the role already offered.

Recommended decision

Keep the role scope intact. If the term RevOps creates concern, adjust the wording. Do not reduce the role to a narrow Head of CRM position after offer.

Preferred title: Head of CRM & Revenue Operations. Acceptable fallback: Head of CRM & Commercial Operations.

The strategy in simple terms

Commercial already contains Sales, Marketing and CRM. The RevOps team is the operating layer that makes those teams work as one system.

It is not a separate department. It is not a new company-wide strategy. It is commercial infrastructure.

Why this matters

Without clear ownership, HubSpot, data, reporting, lead routing, attribution, integrations and handoffs stay fragmented.

The result is more manual chasing, weaker reporting, slower follow-up and lower confidence in commercial decisions.

Proposed operating structure

Sales Owns pipeline, sponsor conversations, deal progression and revenue closure.
Marketing Owns demand generation, campaigns, content, audience growth and marketing performance.
CRM and RevOps Owns HubSpot, data quality, integrations, lifecycle stages, attribution, dashboards, lead routing and commercial process hygiene.

What the Head of RevOps does

  • Turns HubSpot into a trusted commercial source of truth.
  • Defines lifecycle stages such as lead, MQL, SQL, opportunity and customer.
  • Builds lead routing so qualified leads reach the right owner quickly.
  • Owns Sales and Marketing handoffs.
  • Maintains dashboards for pipeline, campaigns, attribution and forecasting.
  • Manages integrations between HubSpot, registration, website forms and email tools.
  • Flags stale deals, broken workflows, missing fields and reporting risks.

What it does not do

  • It does not replace Sales leadership.
  • It does not replace Marketing leadership.
  • It does not create a new company-wide department.
  • It does not take operational control away from the COO.
  • It does not change company strategy.
  • It does not add bureaucracy for its own sake.

Candidate risk

The offer was made for a senior, cross-functional commercial systems role. Changing the title to Head of CRM after offer could look like a downgrade or a narrowing of the mandate.

A title that keeps the wider operating scope protects candidate confidence and reflects the real business need.

Suggested alignment for Monday

Agree the principle: this is a RevOps capability inside Commercial, not a RevOps department.

Agree the scope: CRM, data, integrations, dashboards, lifecycle stages, attribution, lead routing and Sales and Marketing handoffs.

Agree the title: keep Head of CRM & Revenue Operations, or use Head of CRM & Commercial Operations if the RevOps label creates concern.
