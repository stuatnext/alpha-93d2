2. Market Analysis

2.1 The AI Adoption Gap

Singapore’s SMEs are the backbone of the economy but lag significantly behind larger enterprises in the adoption of AI. According to the IMDA, SME AI adoption was 14.5% in 2024, compared with 62.5% among non-SMEs. This represents a documented implementation gap for SMEs.

MOM’s 2026 firm-level survey found that 71.5% of Singapore firms had not adopted AI, with cost and lack of in-house expertise cited as top barriers. Among firms with fewer than 25 employees, adoption was just 23.9%. This confirms that the SME AI gap is an implementation problem.

This means Singapore’s stated ambition (AI integration into 10,000 enterprises) will require both local workforce development and experienced implementation support. The market requires embedded, practical AI operators who can deliver results immediately while training local teams. Strait Up Growth is structured to be exactly that.

2.1.1 Talent Constraints Behind the AI Adoption Gap

Singapore’s enterprise AI targets require both local workforce development and experienced implementation support. The gap is especially acute for SMEs that lack internal AI, data, and transformation teams.

  • Skilled Talent Scarcity: 71% of Singapore employers report difficulty hiring skilled talent. AI model and application development (26%) and AI literacy (25%) are Singapore’s hardest-to-find skills (ManpowerGroup 2026).

2.2 The Fractional Leadership Opportunity

Growth-stage SMEs across APAC face a commercial leadership gap. They are too large for the founder to manage revenue operations alone, but too small to afford a full-time Commercial Director. A fractional model is a practical model for SMEs before revenue scale supports a full-time senior hire, giving them access to senior commercial and AI implementation expertise.

3. Service Offering

Fractional Commercial Leadership

Acts as an embedded Commercial Director for 6 to 12 month engagements, building pipelines, forecasting, and reporting infrastructure that the founder-led sales teams have outgrown.

AI Workflows & Operational Efficiency

Practical operationalisation of existing AI tools into SME workflows. Focuses on agentic workflow platforms, CRM automation, and lead enrichment to recover lost commercial capacity.

Growth, GTM & Market Expansion

Building the go-to-market architecture for market entry or new product launches, providing a deliverable playbook the client's team can run independently post-engagement.

3.1 Client Delivery Model

Each engagement follows a structured delivery process designed for SME implementation.

  • Diagnostic: Review revenue operations, workflows, CRM setup, content operations, and AI readiness.
  • Implementation plan: Define the workflows, tools, data inputs, and commercial metrics to improve.
  • Build and embed: Configure workflows, automate repeatable tasks, and train client teams.
  • Transfer: Leave the client with documentation, reporting dashboards, and operating routines.

11.1 B2B Contract Structure and Disguised Employment Controls

  • The iGaming Idol Ltd. relationship is structured as a B2B services relationship, not employment.
  • Services are or will be documented through commercial contract and statement-of-work documentation, subject to final signed terms.
  • Work is deliverable-based. Strait Up Growth invoices as a Singapore company.

  • The company will retain the ability to serve multiple clients, subject only to ordinary confidentiality and conflict-of-interest terms.
  • Right of substitution or subcontracting applies where contractually permitted.
  • The company retains project documentation, invoices, bank records, and deliverables.
  • The company maintains a multiple-client diversification plan in Year 1.

11.2 iGaming Idol B2B Scope Controls

iGaming Idol Ltd. is an anchor B2B media and events client. Strait Up Growth’s work for this client is limited to B2B commercial strategy, content operations, event/media operations, CRM/process improvement, AI workflow implementation, and technology advisory. Strait Up Growth will not operate gambling services, promote gambling services to players, handle player acquisition, process or receive player funds, sell to players, manage wagering activity, operate betting accounts, or run Singapore-facing gambling campaigns. The engagement is B2B, operational, and advisory in nature.
