Deadline interpretation note

31 July Commercial Readiness Deadline

A project-by-project timeline for reaching commercial readiness by 31 July, covering events, media products, communities and marketing-led commercial projects.

Deadline: 31 July Starting point: 13 May Owners: Project setup + Commercial outputs Applies to all priority projects

Purpose

This document explains what needs to happen by 31 July across project briefs, input packs, commercial strategy, GTM strategy, Sales strategy, Marketing strategy, CRM, collateral, rebooking and execution planning.

It also shows when broader project kickoff meetings need to start. These should not be Commercial-only meetings. Project Owners should set them up, make sure the right people are in the room and capture the required outputs.

Core position

The 31 July deadline should lock the commercial foundation and collateral requirements. It should not force premature final collateral production where products, pricing, packages or delivery boundaries are still unapproved.

Because some setup owners are still learning what “ready” looks like, the Commercial Lead should provide a one-page readiness kickoff checklist to each Project or Product Owner before they run the meeting.

1. What 31 July is for

The 31 July deadline should be treated as a commercial readiness deadline, not a final asset production deadline for every project.

By 31 July, the business should know what is being sold, why it is being sold, who it is for, what it should cost, what the financial ambition is, what the delivery risks are, and what collateral needs to be created.

Where product, pricing and package decisions are approved early enough, final sales collateral can be produced before 31 July. Where those decisions are not approved, the right 31 July output is a collateral brief and production plan, not a fake final deck.

2. Required interpretation

Question Recommended answer Reason
Should project briefs be included in the 31 July deadline? Yes. Commercial needs project briefs and input packs before it can make defensible recommendations on products, pricing, packages, financial assumptions, GTM and execution dependencies.
Should sales collateral be included in the 31 July deadline? Partly. Collateral requirements, sales narrative, positioning and deck structure should be included. Final polished collateral should only be included where product, pricing, package and delivery decisions are already approved.
Should final campaign assets be included in the 31 July deadline? No, unless the project is imminent or already approved. Campaign assets should follow approved GTM, approved pricing, landing pages, tracking requirements and marketing strategy.
Can rebooking or renewal conversations start before 31 July? Yes, but only with guardrails. Soft rebooking and renewal conversations can happen early. Formal proposals need approved pricing, product scope or agreed commercial guardrails.

5. Readiness kickoff checklist

Kickoff meetings should be broader project readiness meetings, not Commercial-only sessions. However, Project and Product Owners may not yet know what “ready” looks like. To avoid vague meetings, the Commercial Lead should provide this one-page checklist before each kickoff.

Checklist item Question to answer in the kickoff Owner to bring answer Required output
Project scope What exactly is the project or product, and what is in / out of scope? Project / Product Owner Clear project definition.
Commercial ambition What revenue, margin, EBITA or strategic outcome are we trying to achieve? Commercial Lead / Finance / Commercial Approver Target or stated assumption.
Existing evidence What data do we already have on sales, pricing, clients, audience, product performance and delivery? Project / Product Owner, Sales Lead, CRM Lead Evidence list and missing data list.
Client and sales insight What are buyers asking for, resisting, renewing, upgrading or rejecting? Sales Lead Sales insight summary.
Product performance What proof do we have that the product actually delivered value? Project / Product Owner, CRM Lead, Event Tech or Media Owner Product evidence summary or known gap.
Delivery feasibility What can we realistically deliver, and where are the operational limits? Project / Product Owner, Partnerships Lead, Production Lead where relevant Delivery constraints and risk list.
Approvals Who needs to approve product, pricing, GTM, collateral, delivery and financial assumptions? Commercial Lead Approval route.
Deadline path What must be done by 31 July, and what can follow after approval? Commercial Lead / Project or Product Owner Project-specific timeline.
Next actions What is missing, who owns it, and by when? Project / Product Owner Action list with owners and dates.
The kickoff is successful only if it produces an owner map, missing input list, approval route, project-specific timeline and next-action list. A meeting that only shares context is not enough.

16. Status definitions

Each project should be given a simple status so leadership can see what is ready, what is conditional and what is blocked.

Status Meaning Action
Green Input pack complete, commercial model approved, GTM framework ready, collateral requirements clear. Move into execution setup and collateral production.
Amber Input pack partial or commercial model close, but one or more approvals, data points or delivery assumptions still need confirming. Proceed with assumptions or conditional approval. Name owner and deadline.
Red Missing critical inputs, unapproved pricing, missing financial targets, unresolved delivery risk or unclear owner. Escalate and resolve before scaling Sales, Marketing or collateral production.
Blue Fast track route approved because speed matters. Track assumption, risk accepted, owner and next checkpoint.

18. Meeting rule

  • Kickoff meetings should be broader project readiness meetings, not Commercial-only meetings.
  • Project / Product Owners set up the meetings and coordinate attendance.
  • The Commercial Lead provides the readiness kickoff checklist before the meeting so setup owners know what “ready” means.
  • Commercial owns the commercial model, GTM framework and commercial recommendations.
  • Brainstorming should happen asynchronously before approval meetings.
  • Ideas should be submitted as recommendations with evidence, impact and owner where possible.
  • Meetings should approve, reject or assign.
  • No open brainstorming in approval meetings.
  • Every blocker gets an owner and deadline.
  • Every meeting ends with a decision log update.

Document owner: Commercial. This deadline note should sit alongside the Project Commercialisation Operating Model and relevant product modules. It is a 31 July interpretation document, not a replacement for project-specific delivery trackers.
