Event module

Event Commercial Strategy Module

A practical module for commercialising conferences, summits, awards, exhibitions, focus events and new event launches.

Sits under the master operating model Applies to events Owner: Commercial Use for annual planning and event cycles

Purpose

This module explains how event projects move from post event evidence to commercial strategy, GTM, sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, rebooking and delivery readiness.

It exists because event products have specific commercial dependencies: venue choice, agenda quality, speaker access, sponsor inventory, ticket pricing, delivery capacity, floorplan, rebooking and onsite proof.

What this module covers

  • What needs to be provided.
  • Who provides it.
  • When each phase starts.
  • How long each phase should take.
  • What needs approval before activity scales.

1. Scope

This module applies to conferences, summits, awards, exhibitions, focus events, retreats, regional events and new event launches.

The master model sets the company sequence. This module explains what that sequence means for events.

The planning depth should change by event size, revenue ambition, strategic importance and risk. The order of decisions should stay consistent.

2. Event planning levels

Event type Planning level Commercial implication
Flagship event Detailed planning Needs full evidence pack, senior approval, full commercial model, monthly ticket and partnerships pacing, full GTM and launch readiness check.
Large regional event Detailed or standard planning Needs product, pricing, sponsor inventory, ticketing, venue and operational assumptions confirmed before Sales and Marketing scale.
Focus event Standard planning Needs sharper buyer focus, leaner inventory, clear sponsor value and shorter sales and marketing timeline.
Awards Standard planning Needs category strategy, sponsor category fit, table sales logic, judging integrity, ticket strategy and ceremony delivery clarity.
New event launch Detailed planning Needs market validation, competitor review, audience proof, category thesis, revenue assumptions and higher scrutiny on risk.
Small tactical event Simplified planning Needs short evidence pack, clear commercial assumption, named approver, basic CRM tracking and simple launch readiness check.

3. Event operating sequence

0. Confirm event type

Agree size, ambition, risk, timeline and planning level.

1. Event evidence

Collect performance, feedback, costs, delivery and market evidence.

2. Commercial model

Set products, pricing, packages, inventory, monthly pacing and financial logic.

3. Event GTM

Define positioning, buyer groups, sales direction, marketing direction and handoffs.

4. Setup

Prepare sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, sponsor delivery and event workflows.

5. Launch and review

Sell, market, deliver, report and feed learning into the next cycle.

5. Non negotiables for events

  • Event products should not roll forward just because they existed last year.
  • Final collateral should not be built before products, pricing, packages and delivery boundaries are approved.
  • Commercial strategy needs Sales, Partnerships, Project Owner, Finance, Conference Content, Marketing, Media and client input.
  • Rory must be involved where event products affect agenda, speaker value, content quality or sponsor integration.
  • Olivia must sign off the sponsor handoff and delivery model before sponsor fulfilment scales.
  • Project Owners sign off execution dependencies. They do not write the GTM.
  • Monthly partnerships sales targets and monthly ticket sales targets are set during the commercial model stage.
  • CRM tracking must be ready before Sales and Marketing activity scales.
  • Rebooking can start softly onsite and post event. Formal rebooking needs approved pricing or guardrails.

6. Stage detail

Stage 0: Confirm event type and planning level

1 to 2 working days

Purpose: Agree what kind of event this is, how commercially important it is, how much planning it needs and who needs to approve decisions.

Inputs required

  • Event type and format
  • Event date or target window
  • Venue strategy or known venue constraints
  • Revenue ambition
  • Expected EBITA or profit contribution
  • Expected costs or major cost changes
  • Strategic importance
  • Commercial risk
  • Delivery complexity
  • Timeline pressure

Outputs

  • Event type confirmed
  • Planning level agreed
  • Approval owner named
  • Required input list
  • Expected timeline
  • Fast track eligibility
  • Known strategic decisions listed, such as venue move or format change

Stage 1: Event evidence and input pack

5 to 10 working days

Purpose: Collect the evidence Commercial needs before products, pricing, packages and GTM are built.

Input Owner Why it matters
What sold, what did not sell, list price versus sold price, discounting and sales objections Sales Lead Shows real market demand, pricing resistance and margin leakage.
Client and sponsor feedback, churn risk, renewal appetite and upgrade potential Sales Lead and Partnerships Lead Shows what needs protecting, changing or upgrading next cycle.
Sponsor delivery issues, fulfilment risk, proof capture gaps and handoff issues Partnerships Lead Prevents Commercial selling products the company cannot deliver properly.
Event timeline, venue, supplier, staffing, production, registration and onsite constraints Project Owner Defines what the event can realistically support.
Agenda performance, speaker access, content gaps, paid content risk and content led product opportunities Conference Content Lead Protects agenda quality and ensures sponsor products do not damage event credibility.
Audience mix, buyer quality, delegate behaviour, ticket buyer feedback and website performance Marketing Lead, CRM Lead, Media and Content Leads Shows whether the event attracted the right people and where conversion can improve.
Expected costs, EBITA expectations, margin assumptions, cash timing and payment risks Finance Lead Protects profit and cash before revenue targets are finalised.
Competitor intel, market movement, pricing benchmarks and buyer behaviour changes Commercial Lead with Sales, Marketing and Content input Shows how the event should be positioned and priced against the market.

Input completion rule

If required inputs are incomplete after the agreed window, Commercial proceeds using named assumptions. Missing inputs become owned risks. Late inputs do not reopen approved strategy unless Stuart, Ana or Pierre confirm material impact.

Stage 2: Event commercial model

1 to 3 weeks

Purpose: Build the commercial foundation for the event before GTM, collateral, CRM and execution scale.

Work covered

  • Event commercial thesis
  • Venue or format strategy where this affects revenue, cost, audience quality or sponsor value
  • Product review
  • Sponsor package review
  • Ticket pricing review
  • Inventory logic
  • Monthly partnerships sales targets
  • Monthly ticket sales targets
  • EBITA and financial health check
  • Margin and expected cost review
  • Discount rules
  • Rebooking and upgrade logic
  • CRM requirements draft
  • Structural revenue leak review

Outputs

  • Commercial thesis
  • Recommended product catalogue
  • Recommended sponsor packages
  • Ticket price ladder
  • Recommended revenue target after products, pricing and major strategy decisions are clear
  • Monthly partnerships sales target
  • Monthly ticket sales target
  • EBITA, margin, cash and expected cost assumptions
  • Approved, provisional and rejected product list
  • CRM requirements draft
  • Risk register and decision log update

Status Meaning

Stage 3: Event GTM strategy

1 to 2 weeks

Purpose: Translate the approved commercial model into clear market direction for Sales, Marketing, CRM, Projects, Partnerships, Conference Content and Media.

Inputs required

  • Approved or near approved commercial model
  • Approved pricing or guardrails
  • Monthly partnerships sales and ticket sales targets
  • Sales account insight, client feedback and competitor intel
  • Audience and ticket buyer insight
  • Agenda and speaker direction from Conference Content Lead
  • Operational constraints from Project Owner
  • Sponsor handoff requirements from Partnerships Lead
  • CRM reporting requirements draft

Outputs

  • Event positioning
  • Target buyer groups
  • Priority sponsor categories
  • Ticket buyer priorities
  • Sales direction
  • Marketing direction
  • Rebooking and upgrade approach
  • Functional KPI direction for Sales Lead and Marketing Lead
  • Handover map
  • Reporting requirements
Project Owners approve the execution dependencies. They do not build the GTM. Partnerships approves sponsor delivery and handoff requirements. Conference Content approves agenda and speaker related product risks.

1 to 3 weeks

Purpose: Turn the approved event strategy into sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, reporting and delivery workflows.

Workstream Owner Output

Marketing strategy Marketing Lead Ticket campaign plan, sponsor demand plan, channel plan, website plan, creative route, audience quality targets and conversion targets.
CRM setup CRM Lead Ticket source tracking, sponsor enquiry tracking, product tagging, rebooking fields, discount tracking, dashboards and handover workflows.
Collateral and enablement Commercial, Sales and Marketing Sales deck, rate card, product one pagers, proposal template, objection handling, ticket one pager and proof assets.
Sponsor delivery workflow Partnerships Lead Sales to Partnerships handoff, Monday.com workflow, fulfilment checklist, proof capture plan and escalation route.
Project delivery workflow Project Owner Critical path, owner map, meeting cadence, production deadlines, registration dependencies and onsite responsibilities.
Content and agenda alignment Conference Content Lead Speaker safe sponsor integration, agenda proof points, content risks, paid content boundaries and event authority messages.
Reporting setup Commercial and CRM Lead Weekly commercial dashboard, ticket pacing, partnership pipeline, buyer quality, discounting, source reporting and risk tracking.

Project specific

7. KPI ownership for events

Event KPIs should follow the approved commercial model. They should not be invented separately by each function.

KPI level Who sets it When it is set Examples
Event level commercial KPIs Commercial Lead, with Commercial Approver and Finance input where needed Stage 2 Revenue target, EBITA target, margin target, monthly partnerships sales target, monthly ticket sales target, discount tolerance, pipeline coverage.
Sales Lead KPIs Commercial Lead Stage 3 Partnerships revenue, monthly close targets, rebooking target, upsell target, pipeline coverage, weighted pipeline, discount discipline.
Marketing Lead KPIs Commercial Lead Stage 3 Ticket demand contribution, lead targets, source quality, conversion targets, buyer quality, website conversion, paid efficiency.
Sales team KPIs Sales Lead Stage 4 Rep targets, account ownership, proposal targets, close targets, renewal targets, pipeline hygiene.
Marketing team KPIs Marketing Lead Stage 4 Campaign output, channel targets, creative testing, lead volume, buyer quality, conversion support, reporting cadence.
Stuart sets KPIs for the Sales Lead and Marketing Lead. The Sales Lead sets KPIs for the sales team. The Marketing Lead sets KPIs for the marketing team.

8. Rebooking timing

Stage Timing Owner Rule
Soft rebooking Onsite and immediately post event Sales Lead with Commercial and Partnerships support Protect the relationship, capture intent, collect feedback and identify upgrade potential. Do not promise unapproved products or pricing.
Rebooking intelligence Within 1 to 3 weeks post event Sales Lead and Partnerships Lead Classify renew, upgrade, at risk, churn, new opportunity and needs senior support.
Formal rebooking After approved pricing or approved guardrails Sales Lead Issue formal offers, renewal proposals and upgrade paths.
Strategic sponsor renewal As early as possible once guardrails exist Sales Lead with Commercial Lead and Commercial Approver where needed Use senior support for high value, sensitive or category defining deals.

10. Collateral timing

Collateral should follow approved products, pricing, packages and delivery boundaries.

Collateral item When it should be ready Dependency
Collateral requirements list During Stage 3 GTM direction and commercial model.
Sales deck structure Stage 3 or early Stage 4 Product direction, positioning and buyer narrative.
Final sponsor sales deck Stage 4 Approved products, pricing, packages and sponsor delivery boundaries.
Rate card Stage 4 Approved pricing and discount rules.
Proposal templates Stage 4 Approved package logic and sales process.
Ticket campaign assets Stage 4 or Stage 5 Approved ticket pricing, landing pages, tracking and marketing strategy.
Proof assets Stage 4 and Stage 5 Media, Content, Partnerships and CRM evidence.
Final collateral should not be forced before products, pricing and delivery boundaries are approved. The brief can be ready earlier. The asset should not be final too early.

11. Approval checkpoints

Approval checkpoint Decision owner Decision required Output
Input readiness Commercial Lead Are inputs complete enough to build the commercial model? Proceed, proceed with assumptions, pause affected decision or escalate.
Commercial approval Commercial Approver with Commercial Lead Are products, pricing, packages, revenue, EBITA, margin, monthly pacing and risk assumptions approved? Approved commercial foundation or required changes.
Execution dependency approval Project Owner, Partnerships Lead, Conference Content Lead where relevant Are delivery dependencies, sponsor handoffs, content risks and fulfilment requirements clear? Approved execution dependencies and named blockers.
Launch readiness Functional leads Are Sales, Marketing, CRM, collateral, team KPIs and reporting ready to scale? Launch approval, conditional launch or blocker escalation.

12. Post event review

The event commercial process should feed the next cycle. This review should happen within 2 weeks of the event closing for major events and within 5 working days for smaller events.

Review area Owner Output
Partnership revenue and pipeline Sales Lead Closed won, lost, churn, renewal risk, upgrade opportunity and pricing resistance.
Ticket performance Commercial Lead, Marketing Lead and CRM Lead Paid tickets, source mix, buyer type, average price, discount use and conversion performance.
Sponsor delivery Partnerships Lead Fulfilment performance, proof capture, delivery issues and renewal risk.
Project delivery Project Owner Operational issues, timeline misses, supplier issues, staffing gaps and critical path learnings.
Conference content Conference Content Lead Agenda quality, speaker performance, content value, sponsor integration issues and authority proof.
Marketing and media performance Marketing Lead, Media and Content Leads Campaign performance, website conversion, content proof, media assets and audience learnings.
Financial performance Finance Lead and Commercial Lead Revenue, EBITA, margin, costs, cash, refunds, FX and payment issues.

13. Meeting rule

  • Pre read required.
  • Brainstorming should happen asynchronously before the meeting.
  • Ideas should be submitted as recommendations with evidence, impact and owner where possible.
  • Meetings approve, reject or assign.
  • No open brainstorming in approval meetings.
  • Every blocker gets an owner and deadline.
  • Every meeting ends with a decision log update.
