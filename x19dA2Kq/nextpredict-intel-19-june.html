Executive read

The useful pattern is now clear. Prediction markets are professionalising through finance, compliance, legal infrastructure and institutional distribution, while the public surface is being shaped by sport, gambling language, Reddit backlash, influencer tooling, scams, bot claims and World Cup volatility.

For NEXTPredict, the commercial opportunity is to own the room between those two worlds. The category does not need another hype summit. It needs a serious market-structure room where exchanges, brokers, market makers, compliance firms, sports data providers, regulators, media, crypto infrastructure and consumer-facing operators can argue through how this scales responsibly.

Themes and conference topics emerging across the sweeps

1. Institutional access and the finance wrapper

The professional story is no longer only Kalshi versus Polymarket. It now includes FalconX, Trading Technologies, Wedbush, Interactive Brokers, ForecastEx, Robinhood, Wealthsimple, CME, Coinbase, Cboe, Schwab and market makers. Finance is trying to make event contracts legible as hedging, price discovery, execution and risk management.

Source trail: LinkedIn X News

Signals:

  • Josh Barkhordar and FalconX publicly tied Kalshi access to institutional price discovery and risk management.
  • Trading Technologies, Wedbush/ION, Interactive Brokers/ForecastEx and Risk Market News all sit in the market-infrastructure layer.
  • CNBC used Kalshi probabilities as macro/Fed expectation content.
  • Acuiti/Finance Magnates signalled derivatives firms are already trading or considering prediction markets.
  • CME litigation against the CFTC around perps widens the definition fight into core derivatives infrastructure.

Possible sessions:

  • From retail odds to institutional event risk: what changes when serious capital arrives?
  • Prediction markets as price discovery, hedge, trade or entertainment product.
  • Clearing, custody, execution and market access: what institutional desks actually need.
  • Where event contracts fit in a derivatives stack.

Named targets and companies: Josh Barkhordar/FalconX, Marco Milano/Trading Technologies, John Needham/Wedbush, Tim Kelly/BitOoda, Interactive Brokers/ForecastEx, Robinhood, CME Group, Cboe, Schwab, Acuiti, SGX, Risk.net.

2. Sports is the adoption engine and the perception trap

World Cup and sport are pulling prediction markets into mainstream attention faster than any institutional thesis. The risk is that the public defines the category as sports betting in a finance wrapper before the broader use cases are understood.

Source trail: News Reddit LinkedIn X

Signals:

  • Business Insider, WIRED, TechSpot and Gambling Insider all connected World Cup traction to sportsbook disruption and category-risk narratives.
  • LinkedIn surfaced DAZN/ADI Predictstreet, German GGL review and European regulator coordination around World Cup-related prediction markets.
  • Reddit threads around Ronaldo crying, whale sports bets and World Cup outright markets show the public-facing surface is already very gambling-coded.
  • X chatter focused heavily on sports volume, Robinhood event-contract volume and Kalshi sports numbers.

Possible sessions:

  • Will sport define prediction markets before finance does?
  • Sports contracts, sportsbook substitution and the economic purpose test.
  • Official data, market integrity and sports-league brand risk.
  • World Cup as prediction markets’ mainstream stress test.

Named targets and companies: Sportradar, ADI Predictstreet, DAZN, Flutter/FanDuel, DraftKings, Betfair, ProphetX, Novig, Sports Illustrated, RotoWire, Business Insider, WIRED, Gambling Insider.

3. Federal versus state regulation is becoming the product roadmap

The category is not waiting for one clean legal definition before scaling. The legal map now includes state challenges, CFTC rulemaking, political-trading bills, European gambling regulators and CME/CFTC litigation.

Source trail: LinkedIn X News Reddit

Signals:

  • Daniel Wallach highlighted Polymarket appellate activity alongside Kalshi cases in Ohio and Tennessee.
  • Reuters Legal reported CME suing the CFTC and chairman Michael Selig over perps classifications.
  • CNBC and LinkedIn posts surfaced Bryan Steil’s bill on members of Congress trading certain prediction markets.
  • European Gaming, Malta Media and LinkedIn legal posts surfaced European regulator coordination and Germany’s GGL review.
  • Reddit discussion repeated state-versus-federal confusion, sometimes with unverified or exaggerated claims. Useful as perception, not source of law.

Possible sessions:

  • CFTC authority, state gambling law and the future of event contracts.
  • The economic purpose test: when does a sports contract serve a public interest?
  • Political markets, public officials and conflicts of interest.
  • What Europe sees that the US does not, and vice versa.

Named targets and companies: Daniel Wallach, Mike Selig/CFTC, Dan Berkovitz, Jonathan Stempel/Reuters Legal, NERA/Katten, Fisher Phillips, IOSCO, GGL, ANJ, state AG/regulatory offices, Gaming Intelligence, European Gaming.

4. Compliance, surveillance and employee conduct are becoming product infrastructure

If employees, journalists, politicians, traders and insiders can take financial positions on real-world events, conduct controls stop being a back-office concern and become part of category credibility.

Source trail: LinkedIn X News

Signals:

  • StarCompliance/Kalshi partnership came through LinkedIn and X as one of the clearest enterprise-infrastructure signals.
  • Solidus Labs and earlier legal/compliance posts support surveillance and market-integrity as a sponsor track.
  • ProPublica’s newsroom ethics angle shows prediction markets creating new conflict policies for media organizations.
  • Fisher Phillips/Mauricio Foeth surfaced employer-policy angles.

Possible sessions:

  • Employee trading policies for prediction markets.
  • MNPI, surveillance and conduct controls for event contracts.
  • Newsroom ethics when news outcomes become tradable.
  • Compliance as a growth enabler, not a legal disclaimer.

Named targets and companies: StarCompliance, Solidus Labs, Hadrius, eflow, ProPublica, Fisher Phillips, Jones Day, Blank Rome, Greenberg Traurig, Katten, NERA.

5. Canada is the cleanest live test of fintech distribution

Wealthsimple/Kalshi is commercially important because prediction markets are entering through a mainstream fintech wrapper, with narrower permitted categories than the US. Reddit reaction shows brand trust damage can arrive before the product even launches.

Source trail: LinkedIn X Reddit News

Signals:

  • Wealthsimple Predict appeared across LinkedIn, X, Gaming Intelligence, CBC-linked Reddit threads and Canadian personal-finance forums.
  • Tal Schwartz framed the revenue rationale by comparing Wealthsimple to Robinhood.
  • Canadian Reddit threads were overwhelmingly concerned with gambling, fintech trust, gamification and whether investing products should sit near event contracts.
  • Commentary noted restrictions around climate, economic indicators and financial markets, with sports/elections/pop culture excluded in Canada.

Possible sessions:

  • What Canada tells us about regulated retail rollout.
  • Fintech trust: what happens when investing apps add event contracts?
  • Product wrapper versus legal wrapper: how users interpret risk.
  • Can prediction markets stay narrow, or does the product inevitably expand?

Named targets and companies: Wealthsimple, Kalshi, Tal Schwartz/North Exit Ventures, Canadian Fintech Newsletter, Canadian Press, CBC, Gaming Intelligence, CIRO and Canadian regulatory/consumer-protection voices.

6. Market microstructure is moving from niche to necessary

The serious trading story is not simply “people predict better”. It is order books, spreads, fees, slippage, latency, inventory risk, market making, adverse selection, arbitrage and resolution risk.

Source trail: LinkedIn Reddit News X

Signals:

  • Lihong Wang/Freeport and Evan Semet surfaced real trader microstructure: 15-minute crypto binaries, toxic flow, UMA/oracle disputes and “free money” that is not free.
  • NYT/actuary Reddit discussion highlighted Ryan Noel, Aidan Gawlowski, OddsJam, Pick the Odds and Bookie Beats in cross-venue arbitrage.
  • Reddit posts on open-source Polymarket market making, BTC up/down efficiency, spread problems and slippage show the retail-builder layer hitting hard execution limits.
  • X and Reddit chatter around bots shows the edge moving toward automation and professional firms.

Possible sessions:

  • What serious traders see that retail users miss.
  • Depth, spread, fees and latency: why the price is not enough.
  • Arbitrage across prediction markets, sportsbooks and exchanges.
  • Market making when contracts resolve to zero or one.

Named targets and companies: Lihong Wang/Freeport, Evan Semet, Ryan Noel, Aidan Gawlowski, Ron Yurko, Nicholas Burgess, Alex Llewellyn, OddsJam, Pick the Odds, Bookie Beats, Susquehanna, BitOoda, Polymarket CLOB builders.

7. Resolution risk is the trust layer everyone keeps rediscovering

Users can tolerate being wrong. They are less forgiving when the outcome process feels unclear, delayed, subjective or manipulable. Resolution ambiguity is now a mainstream product-risk issue.

Source trail: LinkedIn Reddit X

Signals:

  • Krish B. asked who should resolve markets: platform, creator, community or oracle.
  • Freeport/Lihong Wang/Evan Semet discussed UMA/oracle disputes and resolution risk.
  • Reddit scams and Polymarket dispute threads repeatedly returned to verifiable outcomes and whether vague markets invite distrust.
  • X criticism around a US-Iran peace market/UMA dispute showed how quickly settlement concerns become reputation concerns.

Possible sessions:

  • Who decides what happened?
  • Resolution ambiguity as a market-risk premium.
  • Oracle design, disputes and settlement credibility.
  • Why “correct price” does not matter if users distrust the outcome process.

Named targets and companies: Krish B., Chainlink, UMA, Polymarket dispute/community governance experts, Kalshi market operations, Assymetrix, BookRoute, oracle/data infrastructure builders.

8. Crypto/onchain distribution is pushing the risk frontier

Onchain markets are adding leverage, wallets, tokenized assets, copy trading, bots, APIs and composability. That makes distribution faster, but it also drags prediction markets into crypto-scam and wallet-security narratives.

Source trail: Reddit X LinkedIn News

Signals:

  • Base/Reddit threads surfaced PredictEX on Base with leveraged sports prediction markets.
  • Coinbase/Base threads connected tokenized stocks, commodities and prediction markets to “every asset onchain”.
  • Reddit fraud warnings around weatherbot.fi and wallet drains show tool-layer security risk.
  • X/LinkedIn surfaced MEXC, Crypto.com, High Roller, Chainlink, BitMart US/Plaee, Zengo, Hyperliquid and Polymarket-related tooling.

Possible sessions:

  • Prediction markets inside the onchain everything market.
  • Wallet security, API keys and the bot layer.
  • Leveraged sports prediction markets: innovation or avoidable reputational damage?
  • Tokenized assets, perps and event contracts in one retail interface.

Named targets and companies: Base, Coinbase, Chainlink, Hyperliquid, MEXC, Crypto.com, High Roller Technologies, BitMart US, Plaee, Zengo, Vision Wallet, DWF Labs, PredictEX, Poly Follow, FundingPredicts.

9. Media is turning prices into content while also questioning the category

Media outlets are using prediction market prices as editorial data while also reporting on gambling, manipulation, ethics and culture. That creates both credibility and perception risk.

Source trail: News LinkedIn X Reddit

Signals:

  • NYT covered Kalshi and Polymarket viral Knicks marketing moments.
  • Sports Illustrated and RotoWire-style coverage used Kalshi/Polymarket prices as sports-content inputs.
  • Bloomberg, CNBC, WIRED, TechSpot, Business Insider, CoinDesk and ProPublica all pushed different category narratives.
  • Reddit reaction to ads and posts shows a backlash to perceived Polymarket propaganda in non-finance communities.

Possible sessions:

  • Prediction market prices as a new media primitive.
  • When market odds become headlines.
  • The newsroom policy problem.
  • From ad buy to trust buy: why attention alone is not enough.

Named targets and companies: NYT, CNBC, Bloomberg, WIRED, ProPublica, CoinDesk, Sports Illustrated, Business Insider, The Information, Finance Magnates, TechSpot, Gambling Insider, European Gaming.

10. Consumer protection, scams and gambling harm are no longer edge cases

Reddit is brutal but useful. It shows how normal users, finance beginners and crypto communities interpret the category: gambling, scams, fees, wallet drains, whales, bots and people losing money.

Source trail: Reddit News X

Signals:

  • PersonalFinanceCanada, fican and onguardforthee threads around Wealthsimple showed brand-trust backlash.
  • r/Scams and r/CryptoScams threads framed Polymarket-adjacent behaviour through scams and non-verifiable outcomes.
  • Coinbase Reddit users complained about prediction-market fees and questioned why they should trade there.
  • Psychology Today framed prediction markets as something counselors should understand in gambling-like behaviour.

Possible sessions:

  • What responsible scaling looks like when the product feels like gambling.
  • The public will judge the category through the riskiest interface they touch.
  • Fees, suitability and plain-English risk disclosure.
  • How scam narratives attach to legitimate products.

Named targets and companies: Psychology Today/Amanda Giordano, consumer protection voices, Wealthsimple, Coinbase, Kalshi, Polymarket, security/tooling providers, compliance firms, responsible gambling experts.

11. Talent movement proves build mode

Hiring signals, trader podcasts, CMO roles, quant/data science roles and market-maker buildouts show the category is turning into an employment market, not just a product trend.

Source trail: LinkedIn Reddit X

Signals:

  • Limitless Labs, Predictive Labs, Ellie Taylor’s sports prediction markets quant/data scientist role, Blockchain Headhunter and Anastasia Si all surfaced hiring signals.
  • Freeport’s Professional Punters episode with Evan Semet gives a concrete trader-talent example.
  • Reddit side projects and open-source market-making posts show the builder edge, even when quality is mixed.
  • Susquehanna and other quant-firm chatter points to professional market-making competition.

Possible sessions:

  • The prediction markets talent stack: sportsbook, exchange, crypto, quant and compliance.
  • What skills this category actually needs now.
  • From hobby bots to institutional desks.
  • Why talent movement is a better signal than hype posts.

Named targets and companies: Lihong Wang, Evan Semet, Limitless Labs, Predictive Labs, Ellie Taylor, Blockchain Headhunter, Anastasia Si, Susquehanna, Freeport, sports prediction-market startups.

12. Corporate forecasting and decision markets are still the serious-but-quieter opportunity

The original public-good thesis has not disappeared. It is just being drowned out by sports, retail and legal fights. Corporate forecasting, decision markets and information aggregation remain agenda-worthy, especially as a contrast to the sports-volume story.

Source trail: LinkedIn Reddit News

Signals:

  • Shai Novik argued most corporate forecasting is not forecasting.
  • Medium/Decision OS and arXiv market-formation work pushed the category toward decision systems and settlement legibility.
  • Slate Star Codex / FutureSearch-style forecasting threads show conditional forecasting, scenario maps and prediction markets as one input among several.
  • WIRED’s philosopher angle created a useful debate around whether the public-good version can survive the sports version.

Possible sessions:

  • What prediction markets were supposed to do before sports volume took over.
  • Corporate forecasting, decision markets and internal information aggregation.
  • The price is not enough: probabilities, rationale, scenario maps and decision context.
  • Can the public-good argument survive mainstream adoption?

Named targets and companies: Shai Novik, Ade Adegbenro, David Rothschild, FutureSearch, Almanac, Metaculus/Manifold-adjacent thinkers, corporate forecasting practitioners.
