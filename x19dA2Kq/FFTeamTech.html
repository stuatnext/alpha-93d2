Our Design Philosophy

We reviewed a wide range of CRM, lead-generation, AI, and workflow tools to arrive at the leanest practical setup for the Singapore team. Our goal is to eliminate manual work, not create system overhead.

Reduce Manual Admin

Important data should flow seamlessly into the CRM without constant re-entry across multiple spreadsheets.

Improve Lead Discovery

The team should spend more time speaking to the right platform buyers, and significantly less time hunting for them online.

Keep the Stack Lightweight

We are prioritising tools the team can actually adopt and use consistently from Day 1, avoiding bloated IT projects.

A Day in the Life

Testing the conceptual workflow against your real-world B2B scenarios.

Sales

  1. 1.

    You open Apollo to source contact data for acquisition managers at target platforms.

  2. 2.

    Apollo helps source relevant contacts and supports outbound sequencing. When they reply, the opportunity is managed in your HubSpot pipeline.

  3. 3.

    You select the requested titles from the HubSpot Product Library to attach to the deal.

  4. 4.

    You use IMA / WorkBuddy to draft and format the finalized Term Sheet for the client.

Marketing

  1. 1.

    You open the "Marketing Initiatives" pipeline in HubSpot (or your Trello board).

  2. 2.

    You track an upcoming "Asia TV Forum Sponsorship" from Planning to Execution.

  3. 3.

    You check the attached tasks to ensure the B2B partnership pitch decks are finalized.

  4. 4.

    You use IMA to rapidly generate B2B LinkedIn event invites for the sales team to use.

The Handoff

  1. 1.

    Sales marks a deal as "Closed Won" in HubSpot.

  2. 2.

    HubSpot's reporting dashboards update the revenue forecast automatically for the GM.

  3. 3.

    The CRM holds the final commercial record, ensuring all signed documents and title lists are centralized.

  4. 4.

    This clean, centralized record supports a precise handoff summary to the China team to initiate asset delivery.
    (In a future phase, WorkBuddy will be able to auto-generate and send this summary via WeCom.)

Discovery: Over to You

Your answers to these questions will directly shape how we configure the system. Specifically, they’ll determine whether you need one deal pipeline or multiple, which custom fields appear on deal records, and what data flows into the revenue dashboard.

Let's look at what you're doing today.

Commercial Visibility

1. The Blind Spots

Where do you currently lack the most commercial visibility? (e.g., deal status, lead generation, marketing ROI). And specifically on handoffs to China – what’s the thing that most often goes wrong? Missing documents? Wrong rights details? Delayed notice?

2. The Speed Limit

What decisions are currently too slow because the information is scattered or siloed?

3. Leadership Visibility

What does Tim most need to see weekly to feel in control of the commercial operation? (e.g., pipeline value, weighted forecast, deals by category, proposals sent).

The Core Workflows

4. Proposal Generation

How are term sheets and proposals created today, who drafts them, and what part of that process is currently slow or repetitive?

5. The Marketing Reality

What types of marketing/pre-sales work actually need visibility? For those, do you need simple status/ownership tracking, or detailed task management?

6. The Catalog Identity

What is the real role of the title catalog today? Is it currently just a list of titles sales can choose from, or does it need to become a commercial control layer around rights, territories, and exclusivity windows?

System Adoption

7. The Source of Truth

What absolutely must live in one shared system (e.g., deals, contacts, signed contracts, revenue forecasts), and what should deliberately remain outside the core system for now?

8. The Rejection Risk

What would make the team reject this system?

What would make it feel like an annoying admin overhead rather than a sales accelerator?

9. Phase 1 vs Phase 2

What absolutely must be solved in the next 90 days, and what can wait until the team is actually accustomed to using the system daily?

Technical Blockers

10. The Inbox Reality

What email and calendar system do the CRM users use today, and are they willing to standardize if needed? (This determines how seamlessly we can automate email logging and timeline context).

11. IMA & WorkBuddy Readiness

How are you currently using IMA and WorkBuddy? What workflows would you ideally want these tools to handle alongside the CRM and lead gen engine (e.g., drafting, knowledge search, reporting, contract docs)?

What Phase 1 Is Not Trying To Solve

We are deliberately defining boundaries to ensure a fast, successful implementation of the commercial core without creating IT fatigue.

No China delivery workspace (WorkBuddy will make the future bridge far simpler when the time comes)
No full rights / avails conflict engine
No accounting/general ledger system
No heavy project-management tooling
