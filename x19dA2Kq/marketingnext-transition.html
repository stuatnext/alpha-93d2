A practical transition plan to move marketingNEXT from an overextended external-led model into a lean, internally managed community format centred on monthly campaign surgeries.

1. Executive Summary

marketingNEXT should be moved into a simpler, internally managed format. The current model has become too complicated for the level of member traction and commercial maturity: multiple workstreams, fragmented channels, unclear member value, and a cost base that is not yet justified by paying-member outcomes.

The recommended reset is to position marketingNEXT as a monthly peer-led marketing surgery for iGaming marketers. Members submit real campaigns, activations, funnels or marketing challenges, and the group reviews them in a structured, practical session.

Recommended position: Move to a dignified transition with Tara and Ulrich stepping back from day-to-day management, while remaining welcome as members of the community and potential occasional contributors where appropriate.

This approach avoids public drama, gives Tara a respectful landing, recognises Ulrich's written step-back, protects member confidence, and gives Alina a manageable operating model rather than inheriting the current complexity.

Pierre should now lead the second Tara conversation. As Managing Director and someone involved in the creation of marketingNEXT, he is best placed to reset the relationship, acknowledge that the first call landed abruptly, and frame the transition as a leadership decision rather than a personal judgement on Tara.

3. Strategic Decision

What changes

  • External-led project management ends after a transition period.
  • marketingNEXT becomes internally managed by NEXT.io.
  • Multiple workstreams are paused or archived.
  • Member communications are simplified into one primary channel.
  • The programme is centred on one monthly marketing surgery.
  • Paid membership growth is paused until value and cadence are proven.

What stays

  • The marketingNEXT brand and member community remain active.
  • Members continue receiving practical peer-learning value.
  • Tara and Ulrich remain welcome as community members.
  • Marketing campaign review and industry knowledge-sharing remain the core purpose.
  • NEXT.io can still commercialise the community later once the format is proven.
Core proposition: marketingNEXT is a monthly peer-led marketing surgery for iGaming marketers reviewing real campaigns, launches, event activations and marketing challenges.

4. Future Operating Model

Monthly Session: Marketing Surgery

The new format should be practical, repeatable and easy to explain. Each month, members submit a campaign or marketing challenge. Alina selects one or two submissions and facilitates a structured peer review.

Session Segment Timing Purpose
Welcome and framing 5 minutes Set the topic, explain the format, remind members that feedback should be practical and respectful.
Campaign context 10 minutes Campaign owner explains objective, audience, constraints, budget range, channels and results where possible.
Peer review 25 minutes Members critique strategy, creative, channel mix, messaging, funnel, measurement and missed opportunities.
Recommended next steps 10 minutes Group agrees the most useful actions the campaign owner could take next.
Close and next submission callout 5 minutes Confirm next date, invite submissions and capture feedback.

What Alina Owns

  • Member list and community channel.
  • Monthly campaign submission process.
  • Session scheduling and reminders.
  • Selection of campaigns for review.
  • Basic moderation and follow-up recap.
  • Attendance, feedback and engagement tracking.
  • Escalation of commercial opportunities to Sales where relevant.

Recommended time allocation: 4-6 hours per month, excluding the live session. If the model requires materially more time than this, it is becoming overcomplicated again.
