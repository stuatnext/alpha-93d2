Media module

Media Commercial Strategy Module

A practical module for commercialising year-round media products, including News, Podcast, Spotlight and Research Reports.

Sits under the master operating model Applies to media products Owner: Commercial Use for annual planning and product reviews

Purpose

This module explains how media products move from evidence to commercial strategy, GTM, sales strategy, marketing strategy, CRM setup, media kits, delivery workflow and post-campaign review.

It exists because media is not only about rate cards and formats. It is about audience yield: how much commercial value each product creates from its audience, inventory, delivery capacity and client outcomes.

What this module is not

  • It is not a content calendar.
  • It is not an editorial strategy.
  • It is not a production schedule.
  • It is not a rate card by itself.
  • It is not permission for Commercial to sell products that damage editorial trust.

1. Scope

This module applies to year-round media products and commercial media packages.

Media product Includes Commercial question
News Ad banners, newsletter banners, promoted content and PR support. What inventory exists, what sells, what performs, what is risky, and what should be priced or packaged differently?
Podcast Podcast sponsorships, host reads, episode sponsorships, content series and related promotional inventory. What can be sold without overloading production or weakening audience trust?
Spotlight Paid-for video interviews, vlogs and related video distribution packages. What is the value of the format, what proof supports the price, and what delivery workflow protects quality?
Research Reports White papers, sponsored reports, lead-generation reports and report-led campaigns. What topics, sponsors, audiences, lead promises and editorial boundaries make the product commercially credible?
The master model sets the company sequence. This module explains what that sequence means for media products.

4. Non negotiables for Media

  • Media products must be assessed on audience yield, not just rate-card existence.
  • A media product is not commercially healthy if it sells but damages editorial trust, creates low-margin delivery work, produces poor-quality leads or creates makegood liability.
  • Final media kits and sales collateral should not be created before products, pricing, inventory and delivery boundaries are approved.
  • Jake must approve any product that affects editorial quality, audience trust, sponsored content boundaries or editorial standards.
  • Kim must approve media product feasibility, audience proof, inventory capacity and publishing cadence.
  • Lorena must approve any product that creates production load, supplier dependency, talent dependency or delivery risk.
  • Amy or Lorena must confirm client success workflow for the products they own before Sales scales activity.
  • CRM tracking must be ready before paid campaigns, lead generation or media sales activity scales.
  • Commercial should review inventory capacity, sell-through, fill rate, average sold price versus list price, effective CPM or CPL, renewal rate, lead acceptance rate, audience quality, delivery margin, makegood exposure and editorial risk before approving scale.

Media audience yield and commercial health metrics

Metric What it means Why Commercial needs it Primary owner
Inventory capacity Total sellable inventory available by product, channel, format and period. Prevents Sales from overselling limited inventory or underusing high-value inventory. Director of Media
Sell-through rate Percentage of available inventory that is sold. Shows whether demand exists and whether inventory should be expanded, repackaged or retired. Commercial Lead
Fill rate Percentage of available delivery slots or placements that are actually filled and delivered. Shows whether sold inventory is being delivered properly and whether unsold or unused gaps exist. Director of Media
Average sold price versus list price Average realised selling price compared with approved rate-card price. Shows discounting, pricing weakness, package leakage and whether list price reflects market value. Commercial Lead
Effective CPM or effective CPL Realised cost per thousand impressions or cost per lead based on actual delivery and sold price. Allows products to be compared on commercial efficiency and client value, not just headline price. Commercial Lead / CRM Lead
Renewal rate Percentage of clients who renew the same or upgraded media product. Shows whether the product delivers enough value to create repeat revenue. Sales Lead
Lead acceptance rate Percentage of generated leads accepted by the client or Sales as valid and useful. Protects against vanity lead generation and shows whether the audience quality is commercially credible. CRM Lead
Audience quality Relevance and seniority of the audience reached, including job role, company type, geography, buying intent and fit. Determines whether the audience justifies the price and supports sponsor or advertiser ROI. Director of Media
Delivery margin Margin left after production, editorial, client success, design, promotion, tools and delivery time are considered. Shows whether the product is profitable or just busy. Commercial Lead / Finance Lead
Makegood exposure Risk or value of replacement delivery owed because original delivery underperformed, was delayed or was not fulfilled as promised. Shows hidden commercial liability and prevents overpromising future inventory. Client Success Lead
Editorial risk rating Simple risk rating for whether the product could create editorial conflict, credibility issues or brand trust concerns. Protects editorial integrity and prevents Commercial selling products that damage audience trust. Global Editor in Chief

10. Escalation logic

Approval window rule

Approval owners have 5 working days to approve, approve with conditions or reject with required changes. If no decision is received, Stuart escalates to the relevant approver.

Issue Default action Escalates to
Low risk decision delayed Proceed with documented assumption. Commercial Lead
Pricing, EBITA, delivery margin, monthly sales target or revenue decision delayed Escalate. Do not treat silence as approval. Commercial Approver
Editorial trust, sponsored content boundary or audience credibility risk Pause the affected product or package until reviewed. Global Editor in Chief
Production, client fulfilment, makegood exposure or delivery risk Escalate with delivery impact and commercial recommendation. Commercial Lead first. Then Kim, Jake, Lorena, Amy, CRM Lead or Executive Sponsor depending on the issue.
Fast track route requested Record assumption, risk accepted, decision owner and next checkpoint. Stuart, Pierre or relevant decision owner.

11. RAG status: Red, Amber, Green

RAG means Red, Amber, Green. It gives leadership a quick view of whether a media product is ready, at risk or blocked.

Status Meaning Action
Green Inputs complete, audience yield data available, commercial foundation approved, GTM ready, execution setup on track. Proceed.
Amber Inputs partial, yield data incomplete, commercial foundation close, blockers named and owned. Proceed with assumptions or conditional approval.
Red Missing inputs, unapproved pricing, missing financial targets, unclear ownership, missing audience yield data, editorial risk or unresolved delivery risk. Escalate and resolve the affected decision.
Blue Fast track route approved. Track risk, owner and next checkpoint.

14. Meeting rule

  • Pre read required.
  • Brainstorming should happen asynchronously before the meeting.
  • Ideas should be submitted as recommendations with evidence, impact and owner where possible.
  • Meetings approve, reject or assign.
  • No open brainstorming in approval meetings.
  • Every blocker gets an owner and deadline.
  • Every meeting ends with a decision log update.
