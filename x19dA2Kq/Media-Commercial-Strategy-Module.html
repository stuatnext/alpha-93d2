Media module

Media Commercial Strategy Module

A practical module for commercialising news, newsletters, podcasts, video, editorial products, media sponsorships and branded content campaigns.

Sits under the master operating model Applies to media products Owner: Commercial Use for annual planning and media launches

Purpose

This module explains how media products move from evidence to commercial strategy, GTM, sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, campaign delivery and performance review.

It exists because media products have specific commercial dependencies: audience quality, editorial authority, publishing cadence, format inventory, production capacity, sponsor fit, campaign reporting and proof of performance.

What this module is not

  • It is not an editorial calendar.
  • It is not a content strategy document.
  • It is not a campaign delivery plan.
  • It is not a brainstorming document.
  • It is not a replacement for the master operating model.

1. Scope

This module applies to news products, newsletters, podcasts, video series, editorial verticals, sponsored content, media sponsorships, lead generation products, report-led campaigns and new media launches.

The master model sets the company sequence. This module explains what that sequence means for media.

The planning depth changes by revenue ambition, audience maturity, editorial risk, production complexity and commercial importance. The order of decisions should stay consistent.

3. Media operating sequence

0. Confirm media type

Agree product type, ambition, risk, timeline and planning level.

Collect audience, editorial, production, sales and campaign performance evidence.

Set formats, pricing, inventory, monthly pacing and financial logic.

Define positioning, buyer groups, sales direction, marketing direction and handoffs.

Prepare sales, marketing, CRM, media kit, production workflows and reporting.

Sell, publish, distribute, report and feed learning into the next cycle.

5. Non negotiables for media

  • Media products should not be sold without evidence of audience value, delivery capacity and sponsor fit.
  • Editorial authority must not be traded for short term revenue.
  • Final media kits and rate cards should not be created before formats, inventory, pricing and reporting promises are approved.
  • Commercial strategy needs input from Sales, Director of Media, Global Editor in Chief, Production, CRM, Marketing, Finance and Client Success.
  • Jake must approve any product that affects editorial quality, audience trust, sponsored content boundaries or editorial standards. Kim must approve media product feasibility, audience proof and publishing cadence.
  • Lorena must approve any product that creates production load, supplier dependency, talent dependency or delivery risk.
  • Monthly media sales targets and renewal targets are set during Stage 2.
  • CRM and reporting must be ready before sponsor campaigns, lead generation or paid distribution scale.
  • Renewal conversations can start before final collateral, but formal renewals need approved pricing, product scope and proof of delivery.

  • Commercial risk

  • Planning level agreed
  • Approval owner named
  • Required input list
  • Expected timeline
  • Fast track eligibility
  • Known strategic decisions listed, such as new vertical, new show, format change or sponsor category expansion

5 to 10 working days

If required inputs are incomplete after the agreed window, Commercial proceeds using named assumptions. Missing inputs become owned risks. Late inputs do not reopen approved strategy unless Stuart, Ana or Pierre confirm material impact.

Commercial motion Timing Owner Rule

Renewal intelligence Within 5 to 10 working days after campaign report Sales Lead Classify renew, upgrade, at risk, churn, new opportunity and needs senior support.

11. Escalation logic

Approval window rule

Approval owners have 5 working days to approve, approve with conditions or reject with required changes. If no decision is received, Stuart escalates to Ana or Pierre.

Issue Default action Escalates to
Low risk decision delayed Proceed with documented assumption. Commercial Lead
Pricing, EBITA, margin, cash, monthly sales target or revenue decision delayed Escalate. Do not treat silence as approval. Commercial Approver
Cross team conflict Escalate with options and recommendation. Executive Sponsor
Editorial, production, client delivery or reporting risk Escalate with operational impact and commercial recommendation. Commercial Lead first. Then Kim, Jake, Lorena, Amy, CRM Lead or Executive Sponsor depending on the issue.
Fast track route requested Record assumption, risk accepted, decision owner and next checkpoint. Stuart, Ana or Pierre

12. RAG status: Red, Amber, Green

RAG means Red, Amber, Green. It gives Ana and the leadership team a quick view of whether a media product is ready, at risk or blocked.

Status Meaning Action
Green Inputs complete, commercial foundation approved, GTM ready, execution setup on track. Proceed.
Amber Inputs partial, commercial foundation close, blockers named and owned. Proceed with assumptions or conditional approval.
Red Missing inputs, unapproved pricing, missing EBITA or margin targets, unclear ownership, editorial risk, production risk or reporting risk. Escalate and resolve the affected decision.
Blue Fast track route approved. Track risk, owner and next checkpoint.

Jake owns updates where decisions relate to editorial quality, editorial standards or audience trust. Lorena owns updates where decisions relate to production or Podcast and Spotlight delivery. 15. Meeting rule

  • Pre read required.
  • Brainstorming should happen asynchronously before the meeting.
  • Ideas should be submitted as recommendations with evidence, impact and owner where possible.
  • Meetings approve, reject or assign.
  • No open brainstorming in approval meetings.
  • Every blocker gets an owner and deadline.
  • Every meeting ends with a decision log update.
