01 · Overview

Keep the Drive. Fix the rhythm feeding it.

The September Drive is strategically useful as a forcing function and market narrative moment. It should not be where strategy is created from scratch.

What stays the same

September remains the alignment moment

The Drive keeps its role: align the company, package the 2027 vision and prepare the market narrative.

What changes

Planning work starts earlier

Products, pricing, targets, delivery boundaries and GTM inputs need to be shaped before the Drive begins.

What we protect

Teams stop building from assumptions

Sales, Marketing and CRM should build from approved inputs, not reverse-engineer strategy after September.

Core distinction

September Drive

The reveal, alignment and narrative moment. It tells the company and the market where 2027 is going.

Rolling planning rhythm

The work that feeds the Drive. It gathers evidence, reviews products, tests feasibility and prepares execution inputs throughout the year.

✓

Meeting line: I am not questioning the September Drive. I think it is valuable. I want to make sure we do not overload it. The Drive should align and announce the 2027 vision. The planning work needs to happen earlier and in a rolling way because our events and media products all run on different clocks.

02 · 31 July

Define the planning input deadline.

The 31 July point should lock the inputs needed for Sales, Marketing, CRM and execution planning. It should not become a final asset deadline.

By 31 July, clear means
  • Product or package is in the catalogue.
  • Delivery cost model is named for events, including major venue, production, supplier and fulfilment assumptions.
  • Cost-to-serve is named for media products, including editorial, production, distribution and sponsor fulfilment capacity.
  • Price is on the rate card or approved pricing route, based on cost, margin and market value.
  • Revenue target and preliminary margin guardrail are named.
  • Budget and headcount request level is clear: final request, budget envelope, or assumption-based placeholder.
  • Finance provides preliminary guardrails, with final validation following the normal budget cycle.
  • Delivery boundary is signed off by the relevant project owner or functional lead.
  • Collateral requirements are clear enough for Marketing and Sales to brief.
  • CRM and reporting requirements are clear enough to set up tracking.
By 31 July, this does not mean
  • Final polished campaign assets for every project.
  • Final website pages.
  • Final creative concepts.
  • Final sales decks for projects still awaiting approval.
  • Final media production schedules.
  • Every execution detail signed off.
Approval test
3

Approved means four things: the product or package is in the catalogue, the cost and delivery assumptions are named, the price is on the rate card or approved pricing route, and the delivery boundary is signed off by the relevant project owner or functional lead. If those four things are not true, it is not approved.

Cost before price
€

Pricing and targets cannot float above delivery reality: event costs and media fulfilment costs must be visible before pricing, revenue targets or budget requests are treated as decision-grade. If the cost base is unknown, the August presentation should use a budget envelope or scenario, not a final approval request.

Input quality standard
Q

Who checks the standard: the Ops Lead confirms each project's planning inputs meet the defined standard by 31 July. Stuart provides pricing, packaging and revenue inputs, but does not gatekeep other teams' input quality.

Budget-cycle guardrail
F

Finance guardrail: Finance provides preliminary cost, margin and resourcing guardrails by 31 July. Event cost assumptions and media delivery costs must be visible enough to support pricing, revenue targets and budget envelopes. Final budget and headcount validation follows the normal budget cycle. Any material change after 31 July uses the exception path rather than reopening the whole rhythm.

Risk if this slips

Sales starts late

Rebooking, pipeline building and outbound activity lose runway.

Marketing builds from assumptions

Campaigns and collateral need rework once products or prices change.

CRM tracks the wrong thing

Source logic, attribution, lists and reporting get patched after activity starts.

!

Failure-mode line: If we are at risk of missing 31 July, we should know by 21 July. Then we decide whether to reduce the scope, accept less preparation time in August, or move the item into a later cycle.

Critical exception path
!

If August reveals a material issue: only the affected item reopens. The decision owner confirms whether to adjust scope, adjust price, delay launch or keep the approved input with a named risk. The whole September rhythm should not stall because one assumption changed.

03 · 2026 route

This year is compressed. Treat it that way.

Because the current cycle is already in June, 2026 needs a practical route to September. It should not become the template for future years.

June

Map the work

Confirm the project list, year-round products, owner inputs, evidence gaps and decisions needed before 31 July.

31 July

Sign off planning inputs

Agree products, cost assumptions, pricing, packages, targets, delivery boundaries, CRM needs and collateral requirements. This is the handshake point, not the start of feasibility debate.

August

Build execution plans

Sales, Marketing and CRM build from signed-off inputs. August presentations should show execution plans, budget requests and headcount asks at the right fidelity level: final for locked inputs, envelopes for architectural locks and explicit assumptions where detail is not ready.

September

Align and announce

The Drive packages the 2027 vision internally and externally, with Sales, Marketing and CRM working from approved inputs.

August budget and headcount reality
!

Important catch: if budget and headcount requests are expected in August, then the 31 July point cannot be treated as the day planning begins. It must be the day the input quality is clear enough for Sales, Marketing, CRM and Ops to present honest requests. Where inputs are not mature enough, the August output should be a budget envelope or scenario, not a fake final plan.

Scope guardrail

Spring events and year-round products

Need product, delivery cost, pricing, target, package, inventory and delivery boundaries early because sales and marketing runway depends on them.

Later-cycle events

Need an architectural lock by 31 July: audience, broad ambition, route to market, major cost assumptions and delivery assumptions. Final cost base, pricing and packaging should update after the next evidence cycle.

T

2026 triage principle: not every project should be forced to the same depth by 31 July. The honest move is to confirm which items need full locked inputs, which need architectural direction only, and which should move into a later cycle. This protects the August budget and headcount request process from false certainty.

Fallback standard
!

If required inputs are missing: the team should not scramble to invent them. The item either uses the last approved model with agreed assumptions, moves forward with reduced scope, or moves into a later cycle. Missing inputs should create a visible decision, not hidden rework.

04 · 2027 rhythm

Use rolling evidence to feed the next Drive.

The target state is not more process. It is earlier evidence, cleaner decisions and less last-minute reconstruction before September.

1. Review evidenceWithin 2 to 4 weeks after each major event or campaign, capture what worked, what sold, what caused friction and what should change.
2. Shape the portfolioKeep, improve, retire, test or reprice products using evidence from Sales, sponsors, audience, Ops, Media and Finance.
3. Prepare planning inputsProducts, cost assumptions, pricing, packages, targets, delivery boundaries, CRM needs and collateral requirements are prepared before execution planning scales.
4. Confirm feasibilityOps, Finance and Leadership confirm workload, margin, staffing, budget envelope, headcount implications, operational risk and priority trade-offs before the 31 July input standard is treated as locked.
5. Build and announceAugust builds the execution layer. September becomes the alignment and announcement moment, not the month where the strategy gets invented.
✓

Execution principle: For 2027, the goal is to have evidence from each event within 2 to 4 weeks, not months later. That means post-event reviews are scheduled before the event happens, not after. The rolling rhythm continues year-round; the September Drive is its most visible output, not its endpoint.

Alignment principle: Teams keep their ownership. The shared objective is to make dependencies visible early enough that Sales, Marketing, CRM, Ops and Media are not solving the same problem separately.

Single-view capacity check
C

Capacity principle: events, retreats and year-round media should be checked in one view before September plans are finalised. Shared constraints include design, CRM, sponsor delivery, content production, email inventory, social output, website updates, leadership attention and headcount. Moving planning earlier does not add work; it moves work out of the September-October crunch. If capacity is a constraint, discuss it now, not in August.

Behaviour standard
✓

No surprises at the Drive: teams should not present assumption-based plans as approved plans. If an input is not approved, it should be marked as an assumption, a risk or a later-cycle item. The three decisions should be reviewed monthly in the planning review, and non-adherence should be treated as a planning risk, not a personal failure.
