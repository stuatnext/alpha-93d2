The proposed role

A featured Day 1 role focused on whether prediction markets are becoming a real investable category, where the economics sit, and what public-market investors should be watching.

NEXTPredict already has operators, exchanges, legal, enforcement, media and analyst voices in the room. The gap we would like you to fill is the investor and public-markets perspective: direct, commercially curious and willing to test the thesis rather than cheerlead the category.

Recommended headline session

The Prediction Markets Trade: Robinhood, Retail Attention and Who Captures the Economics

Format: 25-minute featured fireside on 22 October.

Core angle: prediction markets are being pulled into public-market narratives, broker strategy, sports distribution and retail attention. The useful question is whether this becomes a durable revenue pool or a temporary market story while the category is hot.

Why this works: it connects directly to Robinhood, retail trading, investor psychology, market structure and media attention without treating prediction markets as a closed insider conversation.

Questions the session can own

  • Is prediction-market revenue durable, or event-driven attention with a better multiple?
  • Does Robinhood-style distribution win, or do exchanges and market makers keep the economics?
  • Are sports contracts the adoption engine or the perception trap?
  • What should investors watch before believing the category thesis?
  • Where does pricing power sit: users, liquidity, data, clearing, compliance or media?
  • What breaks first: regulation, liquidity, settlement, bad marketing, consumer risk or simple boredom?

  • The session can produce clips and follow-up content that your audience will understand.
  • If you moderate, you help shape who is on the panel.

Commercial structure options

We know your time has value, so we should structure this properly. The cleanest route depends on the deliverables you are comfortable with.

Flat appearance fee

You quote your standard rate. Deliverables could include the featured fireside, moderated panel, curated meetings and limited approved promotion.

Base fee plus ticket upside

A base fee plus a tracked commission on paid tickets sold through an agreed link. A sensible range to discuss would be 10% to 15% of net ticket revenue.

Sponsor-backed finance package

A branded investor briefing, private lunch or content segment around your role. Sponsor revenue underwrites the economics, with disclosure handled cleanly.

Sponsor-introduction upside

For warm introductions that close, we can discuss a success fee on collected net sponsorship revenue. A sensible range to discuss would be 5% to 10%, with written terms.

Commercial next step: agree the preferred structure first, then lock deliverables, panel format, approvals and any promotional commitments in writing.
