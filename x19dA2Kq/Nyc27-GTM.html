Commercial · mission and positioning

The commercial decision is made

NYC 2027 is a lift-and-shift of the proven 2026 product set, revalued at a Manhattan premium. The team should stop re-debating pricing and start selling against the approved rate card, the deal desk and the scarcity story.

The model

Scarcity, not square metres

European competitors scale to 30,000+ delegates and commoditise floor space. We sell a capped premium room: 20-stand max floor, max 2 stands per brand, multi-day format engineered around a c.1,500 peak-capacity venue environment.

The proof

51.4% executive density

Verified 2026 registration data: 30.4% Founders/MDs plus 21% C-Suite. Operators 28.6%, suppliers 22.6%. This concentration of decision-makers is what justifies premium pricing against mass-market events.

Strategic continuity

Lift and shift, repriced

The 2027 physical product set mirrors 2026 to protect operational bandwidth for the NEXTPredict launch. The valuation does not: historically underpriced inventory is rebalanced to reflect genuine scarcity, buyer density and New York delivery value.

What it is not

Not a discount-led floor

Not a volume expo, not a CPM branding play, not a stand bazaar. No steep discounting culture: 15% hard ceiling, 5% on power inventory, sweeteners instead of price erosion.

Mission line: We are selling the densest executive room in the industry's North American calendar — and pricing it like we believe it.

Media · editorial

Adjacent, independent, editorial

NEXT.io Media — news, podcasts, reports, newsletters under Kim — is editorially separate from the events business. Media is not tasked with event delivery: no proof packs, no conversion pages, no campaign production, no sales collateral. Those live with Marketing and Event Ops. What media chooses to cover, it covers on editorial merit.

The boundary

Editorial is not a delivery function

Proof packs belong to Event Ops & Partnerships. Conversion pages, launch campaigns and sales kits belong to Marketing. Media's independence is what makes its coverage worth having — for readers, and for the event's credibility when coverage does happen.

The interface

Pitch, don't task

Marketing and commercial can pitch stories to media like any source would: the category thesis, the agenda, confirmed speakers, the density data. Media decides what runs. No guaranteed coverage, no disguised advertorial — sponsored placements are commercial media inventory, a separate (and currently unbuilt) rate-card workstream outside this GTM.

The opportunity

Stories that are genuinely good

NYC 2027 will generate real editorial material: the North American premium-event thesis, speaker interviews worth running on their own merit, market analysis from the room, post-event takes. When the event is newsworthy, coverage follows naturally — that is the only reliable mechanism.
