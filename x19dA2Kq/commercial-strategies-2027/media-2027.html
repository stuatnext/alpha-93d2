How the two routes are priced: Exclusive is the priority and the easy single sale. Headline sits just below Exclusive (Tier 1: EUR 80k vs EUR 90k), so a serious buyer choosing between them upgrades to Exclusive for a small step up and locks out all competitors. Supporting is the accessible entry tier. Headline + 2 Supporting always totals more than Exclusive (Tier 1: EUR 150k vs EUR 90k), so we are never worse off selling the category shared, and no buyer can quietly assemble all three slots more cheaply than the Exclusive. To the client, Exclusive reads as strong value: the whole category for EUR 90k, versus EUR 150k if three sponsors shared it. Exclusive is sold annual or minimum quarterly and includes 3 to 4 promoted articles per quarter.

Banner prices are set from the first-half 2026 impression and click data. The pop-up has by far the highest click-through rate on the site (~4%), which supports holding it at EUR 8,000/month as the exit-intent unit. The skyscraper moves to EUR 3,500/month on its 8-slot rotation. The site-wide sticky moves to a 10-slot rotation at EUR 8,300/month, lower per slot than the prior single-position price, so the year-long banner clients fit without losing impressions; the base models 3 firm sticky sells of the 10 slots. Banners are sold monthly, so available inventory is full-year rotation capacity. Ads can now also run on event pages.

7 · What we agreed on 24 June

  • Banners move by hybrid transition, not a hard cut. Transition the Mega Banner and leaderboard clients into the Site-wide Sticky. Raise the sticky rotation to 10 slots and price it at EUR 8,300/month per slot, lower per slot than the prior single position, so more year-long clients fit without losing impressions and churn is reduced.
  • Keep the Skyscraper as a static unit that rotates on page load, on its 8-slot rotation, priced at EUR 3,500/month. It sold well this year (Fast Track, 1XBet, Square Ventures, Pragmatic) and keeps a like-for-like home for those clients while helping page speed.
  • Add an Exit-intent Pop-up that fires when a visitor goes to leave, not on load. The pop-up has the highest click-through rate on the site (~4%), so it holds its EUR 8,000/month value as the less-intrusive successor and is the like-for-like home for pop-up buyers.
  • Lighten the rest of the site to improve page speed and prepare it to carry affiliate content. Ads can now also run on event pages.
  • Category sold as Exclusive or Headline + Supporting, Exclusive as the priority. Headline is priced just below Exclusive so buyers upgrade to it, and Headline + 2 Supporting always tops the Exclusive so the shared route never costs us. Exclusive sold annual or minimum quarterly, with 3 to 4 promoted articles per quarter.
  • NEXT Predict gets its own product lines (site-wide sticky, category, newsletter) at lifted introductory H1 pricing, reassessed and raised for H2 as traffic and the summit build. Its podcast sponsorship sits in the Podcast line.
  • NEXT Predict stays clean. No iGaming or casino headliners (Pierre's rule), sold by a separate predict sales team. The build is slower because we cannot cross-sell it into existing gambling accounts, so revenue is conservative.
  • Podcast lifted, not declining. The NEXT Predict podcast is in the base; Connor's podcast is kept as upside; interview demand off Pierre's show moves to Connor's podcast or Spotlight.
  • Brazil, LATAM and crypto stay as upside, not in the base, until there is enough data to project them.
