3 · Project-level targets and sell-through logic

The EUR 2.60M Media target is the sum of four project-level targets. Each project line needs enough sellable inventory to hit its own target without assuming 100% sell-through. The model shows target revenue against a larger available product pool, allowing for churn, unsold inventory, timing delays and product substitution.

Project accountability: the total Media target is not a replacement for project-level targets. News cannot simply make up for a weak Podcast number unless Ana explicitly agrees the target is pooled. Each project line has its own sellable inventory and forecast.
Pipeline coverage by project line: Will should not only show pipeline against the total EUR 2.60M. He should show coverage by project line, News against EUR 1.876M, Podcast against EUR 224k, Spotlight against EUR 300k, Research against EUR 200k. A large News pipeline does not automatically solve Podcast, Spotlight or Research unless Ana agrees the target is pooled. Historically the business has worked with project-level revenue lines, so the 2027 plan should do the same.

Category inventory is modelled by category capacity, not by adding routes together: Exclusive and Headline + Supporting are alternative monetisation routes for the same category, never additive. The product-level detail (price, available inventory and what sales must sell) is in section 5.

How to read this: Available = sellable inventory at list price. Inventory value = that inventory in EUR. To sell = units sales must close to hit the line target. Target revenue = the EUR those units bring in. The total Media target needs ~75% sell-through of the available pool, not 100%.

Category rule: a category is sold as Exclusive OR as Headline + Supporting, never both at the same time. The inventory above counts each category once, at its best available route, so the two routes are never added together. Tier 2 shows this directly: of 5 categories, 3 sell as exclusive, 1 as Headline + Supporting, 1 stays unsold.
Capacity check: available inventory is not the same as guaranteed delivery. Kim must confirm delivery capacity by line, especially Spotlight, Research and Content & PR. If capacity is lower than the pool above, reduce the sellable inventory or raise pricing rather than over-promise.

Research Reports are standalone in 2027, following their standalone 2026 target. The category rate card by tier and the new-shows upside lines are in the Appendix.

6 · Key risks and dependencies

Foreign-language sites: the EUR 2.60M target assumes NEXT.io's added resources, AI investment and new foreign-language sites create more sellable media surface. If Brazil, LATAM/Spanish and Brazilian Portuguese are delayed, not packaged, not measurable or not owned commercially, EUR 2.45M remains the conservative fallback.
  • Spotlight capacity: the ~445k inventory and EUR 300k forecast assume ~34 outputs. Available inventory does not mean unlimited delivery, Kim must confirm what can be delivered at quality and margin; if lower, reduce the inventory pool or raise pricing.
  • Podcast supporting demand: supporting sponsorship has historically been harder to sell. Podcast should not be pushed higher unless Will validates demand, because paid guests and paid episodes are being removed and cadence is reducing. If demand is thin, Podcast sits nearer EUR 110-150k.
  • Banner-client churn: 38 advertisers worth ~EUR 865k must migrate to premium inventory. Premiumisation means fewer, higher-value sponsors over time; the goal is to retain the right clients, not every buyer. Migration plan in the Appendix.
  • Sales capacity: media sales is not currently a dedicated hire. Confirm whether existing capacity supports EUR 2.60M, by project line, or whether dedicated ownership / a hire is needed.
