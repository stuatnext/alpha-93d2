5 · Key risks and dependencies

Foreign-language sites: the EUR 2.60M target assumes NEXT.io's added resources, AI investment and new foreign-language sites create more sellable media surface. If Brazil, LATAM/Spanish and Brazilian Portuguese are delayed, not packaged, not measurable or not owned commercially, EUR 2.45M remains the conservative fallback.
  • Spotlight capacity: EUR 300k assumes ~34 outputs across the year. Kim to confirm deliverable at quality and margin; if not, raise pricing, narrow deliverables, or lower the target.
  • Podcast supporting demand: supporting sponsorship has historically been harder to sell. If demand for the two EUR 57k packages isn't there, Podcast sits nearer EUR 110-150k (headline-led).
  • Banner-client churn: 38 advertisers worth ~EUR 865k must migrate to premium inventory. Premiumisation will mean fewer, higher-value sponsors over time; the goal is to retain the right clients, not every buyer. Migration plan in the Appendix.
  • Sales capacity: media sales is not currently a dedicated hire. Confirm whether existing capacity supports EUR 2.60M or whether dedicated ownership / a hire is needed.

6 · Costs, salaries and margin guardrail

  • 2025 is a margin benchmark, not a cost benchmark. 2027 costs will be materially higher (team has grown; more hiring possible).
  • Salaries must be included. EUR 258k is only a direct-cost view until Kim and Finance confirm the full basis.
  • Approve EUR 2.60M with a salary-inclusive cost envelope and a 60%+ all-in margin guardrail.
