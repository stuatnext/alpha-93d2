02 Where marketing sits · the Commercial Charter

Marketing is the growth engine inside Commercial.

The Commercial Department owns the complete revenue engine of NEXT.io: it unifies Marketing, Sales and Revenue Operations into one performance-driven system that converts brand influence into predictable, scalable growth. Marketing reports into Commercial so the whole engine is accountable as one.

Pillar · Marketing

Influence & demand

Brand positioning and authority, demand generation, audience growth within target segments, marketing-sourced & influenced pipeline, campaign ROI and distribution across owned and paid channels. This is yours.

Pillar · Sales

Revenue closure

Revenue closure, forecast accuracy, pipeline progression, margin discipline, and client expansion and retention. Sales converts qualified demand into revenue. Led by Will.

Pillar · RevOps

The infrastructure

CRM architecture and integrity, lifecycle & funnel-stage governance, attribution, dashboards, forecasting, marketing automation and data hygiene. RevOps owns the system; marketing uses it well.

The revenue lifecycle Commercial owns

Market awareness❯ Audience engagement❯ Lead generation❯ Qualification & progression❯ Sales conversion❯ Expansion & retention

How we operate · performance principles

  • Revenue impact is the primary measure of success.
  • Influence and revenue must compound together.
  • Campaigns require defined objectives and measurable outcomes.
  • Data informs decision-making.
  • Underperformance is addressed promptly.
  • Ownership is explicit and measurable.
  • Cross-functional alignment is mandatory.
  • Execution is delegated; outcome accountability sits with commercial leadership.
❯

The principle that runs through everything: influence is treated as a measurable commercial asset. It lowers acquisition cost, strengthens sponsor and partner retention and pricing power, shortens the sales cycle and builds long-term resilience. Revenue growth that isn't backed by strengthening influence is considered fragile and unsustainable.

03 Why this role exists

Marketing as an engine, not a support function.

The catalyst for the restructure was Nico stepping away from the Marketing Director role proposed to him in September 2025. He was honest about where his strengths lie. Rather than hire into the old setup, we treated it as a chance to design the architecture properly, so we scale deliberately rather than reactively.

Marketing at NEXT.io cannot run as a reactive service desk. It must be a strategic growth engine that sets the narrative, drives visibility, spots opportunities, builds authority systematically and converts influence into revenue. We sell marketing and growth services to the industry, so we should be demonstrating the gold standard ourselves.

The principles the structure is built on

  • Marketing reports into Commercial for unified revenue accountability.
  • Sales, Marketing and RevOps operate as one aligned system.
  • Influence is a measurable asset that compounds over time.
  • Brand authority and commercial performance strengthen each other.
  • Designed around functional excellence, not product silos, to scale.
  • Ownership is explicit; underperformance is addressed promptly.
❝

On Nico: he was offered this Director role and, after real reflection, declined in February 2026, because the strategic and process-driven side isn't where he does his best work. He moves to Head of Event Marketing & Brand and becomes your bridge in: helping you settle, read the culture and navigate the team, and covering marketing leadership when you're away. This isn't a step backwards; it's putting people where they deliver the most value.

04 Your role, defined

A strategic operator who could grow into a CMO.

You own the full marketing strategy across events, media, brand and commercial campaigns: comfortable presenting and defending strategy, owning budget and ROI, setting performance standards, managing the team functions and bridging influence and revenue. This is a leadership role focused on strategy, systems and team performance, not hands-on campaign execution.

Core responsibilities

  • Own the annual marketing strategy across events, media, research and future verticals.
  • Define positioning themes aligned to company objectives.
  • Build the marketing calendar in coordination with sales priorities.
  • Drive marketing-sourced and influenced pipeline growth.
  • Align campaigns with revenue targets; monitor conversion.
  • Oversee channel strategy: social, email, SEO, paid, PR.
  • Lead and develop the team with clear accountability.
  • Own the budget and resource allocation.
  • Champion AI-enabled marketing efficiency and intelligence.

How success is measured

Commercial impact

Marketing-sourced pipeline, ticket-sales contribution, conversion-rate improvements and paid-channel ROI.

Influence & authority

Qualified audience growth, repeat sponsors and partners, share of voice, direct & branded-search growth, inbound commercial enquiries.

Operational excellence

On-time campaign execution, reporting discipline, team performance uplift, and a reduction in reactive work.

The accountability framework

The role is a senior hire, so the plan ties it to measurable return. Three mechanisms are worth knowing about now:

Break-even

Generate a verifiable increase in marketing-sourced pipeline that offsets the role's fully loaded cost within the first two quarters. Reviewed at the end of Q2.

LTV / CAC

Establish and improve lifetime value vs acquisition cost by segment and channel, reported quarterly. The proof that influence is a commercial asset, not a vanity metric.

EBITDA protection

Scaling (specialist hires, splitting Brand from Events) is gated by performance. Your bonus is tied to commercial KPIs and company EBITDA.

❯

The one early, formal deliverable: within your first 30 days the plan asks you to define a set of baselines with Sales and RevOps: current marketing-sourced and marketing-influenced pipeline, delegate & ticket volume attributable to campaigns, and cost per acquisition by channel. That's what the break-even target is calculated from.

My steer: that's the formal frame. In practice we'll build it together and there's no rush in week one. I'm flagging it so nothing here is a surprise, not to put a clock on you.
