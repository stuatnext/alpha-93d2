Executive decision

The recommendation

Move Valletta 2027 to a value-led pricing model. Keep 2026 prices only for identical-scope rebookers. Apply 2027 pricing to all new buyers, upgrades, larger footprints, exclusivity and new activations.

Commercial logic

Why prices need to move

2026 proved demand

  • €1.87m achieved against a €2.19m target.
  • 3x3 stands sold at high volume, around 47 units.
  • Content participation and track products sold in meaningful volume.
  • Networking and experiential products showed repeatable demand.

2026 also exposed underpricing

  • Lanyard sold at €20k while comparable anchors sit far higher.
  • Space-only and premium floor assets left pricing headroom.
  • Custom and side-event products need commercial discipline because costs change by venue and format.
  • €0 and vague line items make revenue reporting unreliable and weaken negotiation control.
Commercial principle: protect volume products, push scarce assets harder, and stop treating premium visibility as a cheap add-on.

Rules

Pricing rules for 2027

Rate-card structure

  • Rebook: 2026 price for identical scope only.
  • 2027 list: public anchor for new sales and upgrades.
  • Floor: lowest price before director approval.

Commercial controls

  • No single discount above 10% without director approval.
  • Cumulative discounts capped at 15%.
  • No custom activation without Ops approval.
  • No panel without Content approval.
  • No vague Misc line items.
  • No sponsor asset should be delivered for €0 unless it is explicitly included in a signed sponsorship package.

Rebooking

Rebooking policy

Do not reopen 2026 pricing as a general discount. Rebook pricing is a loyalty protection mechanism for identical scope.

Allowed at 2026 pricing

  • Same sponsor.
  • Same product.
  • Same scope.
  • Signed inside the rebooking window.

Moves to 2027 pricing

  • Additional products.
  • Larger booths.
  • Exclusivity upgrades.
  • Custom activations.
  • Renamed or materially expanded products.
Future discipline: 2028 rebooking should reward early commitment, not preserve outdated pricing. The guardrail is set out on the next slide.
Future pricing discipline

2028 rebooking guardrail

Rebooking should reward speed and loyalty, not preserve old underpricing. The next cycle needs an approved future rate card before onsite rebooking opens.

Future rule

  • Approve 2028 pricing before Valletta 2027 starts.
  • Open rebooking onsite, but anchor every renewal against the approved 2028 rate card.
  • Offer a defined rebook incentive for same product and same scope only.
  • Make the incentive expire 30 days after the event.

What does not carry over

  • No automatic rollover of 2027 list pricing.
  • No legacy pricing on upgrades, larger footprints or exclusivity.
  • No automatic discount on scarce, underpriced or operationally complex assets.
  • No verbal exceptions without written commercial approval.

Risk

Risks and controls

Commercial risks

  • Existing sponsors expect 2026 pricing beyond identical scope.
  • Sales discounts new pricing back toward 2026 levels.
  • Diamond and new activation revenue does not materialise.
  • Premium activations create ops cost or delivery risk.
  • Content inventory sells beyond what Rory can protect.

Controls

  • Written rebook rule and deadline.
  • Floor price approvals.
  • Ops approval before custom activation sale.
  • Content approval on all paid speaking products.
  • Post-event ROI reporting for Platinum and Diamond.
Decisions

Decisions required

Commercial decisions

  • Confirm €2.35m sales partnership target.
  • Approve the 3-layer model: rebook, list and floor.
  • Approve Diamond at €120k plus.
  • Choose lanyard model: exclusive at €55k or split at 2 x €30k.
  • Confirm badge strategy: single sponsor or segmented badges.
  • Approve ticket entitlement formula: 1 Full Event Pass per €8k net spend.
  • Approve the 2028 rebooking guardrail so future renewals use the future rate card, not the previous year's list price.

Operational decisions

  • Confirm which new activations are deliverable.
  • Set content capacity by stage and format.
  • Merge duplicate HubSpot product records.
  • Retire or rename vague products, including Misc and DO NOT USE items.
  • Confirm cost base and target margin.
