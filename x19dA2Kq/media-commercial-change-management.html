NEXT.io Media, 2027 transition

Media transition brief

The 2027 media model needs to turn scattered product buying into clearer annual partnerships. The plan should give sales one story, give delivery one rule set, and give priority clients a bridge from what they bought before into a cleaner model.

Broad strategy first Client migration logic No invented inventory numbers Actions to close the plan

Executive read

The strategy works if the transition is sold as consolidation, not replacement. Clients should not hear, "you used to buy this, now buy this other thing." They should hear, "your existing activity has been consolidated into a stronger annual media position with clearer ownership, better storytelling and better proof of value."

What is strong

The direction is commercially right

Moving away from low-quality ad clutter gives sales a more premium story and gives the media team more control over quality.

What needs work

The bridge needs to feel specific

Priority clients need account-level migration plans. A generic product map will not protect Pragmatic, BetConstruct, SOFTSWISS or other large accounts.

What comes next

Product rules before sales launch

The product list, rights, category rules, rotation logic, reporting standard and legacy exit rules need to be settled before sales starts renewal conversations.

Core point: 2027 should be framed as the shift from buying media placements to owning a clearer market position across editorial, video, newsletter, research and site visibility.

Sales framing

Sales needs the story before the product sheet. The product sheet supports the story. It should not become the story.

"We are moving from fragmented media placements into annual media positions. You will get fewer disconnected assets and a clearer platform for category presence, thought leadership, distribution and performance reporting."

Old sales motion

  • Renew the same banner.
  • Sell a podcast slot.
  • Add Spotlight as a bolt-on.
  • Use PR support or articles to close gaps.
  • Discount placements when value is unclear.

New sales motion

  • Map the client’s current media behaviour.
  • Identify the buyer outcome they care about.
  • Move them into one primary 2027 lane.
  • Add supporting products only where they strengthen the lane.
  • Use quarterly reporting to protect renewal value.
Sales rule: no priority account should receive a product list first. They should receive a bridge from their current buying pattern to a 2027 position.

Priority client transition view

The goal is not to give every client the same recommendation. The goal is to protect revenue while moving each client into the lane that matches their real buying behaviour.

Pragmatic Group

Podcast headline exclusiveConnected With / SpotlightResearch and reportsMedia and banners

Current pattern

Pragmatic has bought recurring authority products, not only media placements. The relationship sits around podcast ownership, Spotlight / Connected With storytelling, research/report association and supporting media visibility.

2027 bridge

Build a consolidated Multi-Media Authority Partnership. The centre should be podcast and Spotlight continuity, with research as the intelligence layer and newsletter/site distribution as the support layer. This is not a banner migration. It is an annual authority platform.

Why this works

The podcast transition creates risk if the conversation focuses on episode count. The safer commercial story is format ownership, senior audience access, continuity of Connected With, distribution and quarterly proof.

Suggested move: create a bespoke Pragmatic bridge proposal with two options. Option one protects the existing podcast and Spotlight value. Option two expands into research and distribution, with stronger reporting and fewer fragmented placements.

BetConstruct

€328k across 2025 and 2026Mega bannerPodcastSpotlightMagazineContent

Current pattern

BetConstruct has the broadest clean media footprint. They have bought site visibility, content, podcast, Spotlight and magazine. This suggests they value sustained market presence more than a single format.

2027 bridge

Move them toward a Global Markets Anchor or Category Headline position, supported by Spotlight or podcast content and quarterly performance reporting.

Why this works

This gives sales a premium alternative to renewing a basket of ad formats. It also gives media a way to reduce clutter without reducing perceived value.

Suggested move: define whether BetConstruct gets first conversation on a broad Global Markets position or a narrower category lane. Do not offer the same scarce lane to other platform accounts until this is resolved.

SOFTSWISS

2025 Mega Banner2026 bannersPR supportBacklink contentPerformance scrutiny

Current pattern

SOFTSWISS has bought high-visibility placements and has shown interest in performance detail. They will not accept a premium migration without proof.

2027 bridge

Move them into a proof-led visibility package. This could sit as Category Headline, Sticky Banner or an Emerging Verticals lane where the fit is credible, supported by quarterly reporting.

Why this works

The bridge protects the commercial value of banners while giving the client a cleaner reason to pay for fewer, stronger placements.

Suggested move: prepare the SOFTSWISS plan with a sample performance report. Lead with visibility quality, audience fit and reporting, not the removal of old banners.

Playtech

2026 Mega BannerPlatform accountPremium visibility replacement

Current pattern

Playtech looks like a cleaner banner-transition account. The risk is simpler than Pragmatic. They need a better replacement for premium web presence.

2027 bridge

Move them into Category Headline, Sticky Banner or Global Markets support, depending on exclusivity availability.

Why this works

The bridge is easy to explain. The old product was premium visibility. The new product should be premium visibility with better context and less clutter.

Suggested move: build a direct old-to-new value comparison. Keep the story simple and avoid overcomplicating the account with unrelated products.

Evolution, Imagine Live and other content-led suppliers

PodcastSpotlightMega bannerMagazineVisual storytelling

Current pattern

These accounts have stronger fit with storytelling, product launches and executive visibility than with generic site ownership.

2027 bridge

Move them toward Spotlight Studio, Podcast Theme Sponsorship or Multi-Media Partner packages, with distribution attached.

Why this works

It protects value if paid guest participation changes and gives suppliers a richer format for product narrative.

Suggested move: create one supplier storytelling template. Use it for Evolution, Imagine Live and similar accounts, then tailor by category.

Hub88, Stake, Plaee and mixed relationship accounts

Mixed event and media exposureNewsletterSpotlightBannersStrategic relationship value

Current pattern

These accounts risk being misread if media and event value are blended too loosely. Some have strong strategic value but not all of it belongs in the media plan.

2027 bridge

Create two-lane account plans. One lane covers media products. The other covers event and relationship value. The narrative connects them, but the commercial ownership stays clear.

Why this works

It protects the media strategy from inflated assumptions and gives sales a cleaner way to package strategic accounts.

Suggested move: include these accounts in transition planning, but avoid treating their full relationship value as media spend.

Final commercial position

The commercial move is not to replace every banner with a new banner. The move is to convert product buyers into annual media partners.

The strongest 2027 plan will protect revenue, reduce site clutter, create cleaner premium inventory and give sales account-specific bridges for the biggest clients.

Best next step: finalise product rules and mock-ups, then build account bridge plans for the priority clients before the sales workshop.
