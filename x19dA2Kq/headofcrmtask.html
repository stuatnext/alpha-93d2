Where the real complexity lives

Anyone can spot a duplicate. We care most about the problems specific to an events + media business — the ones with no button in HubSpot. These are what a great answer goes after:

  1. Renewal & retention across an annual event portfolio. The same sponsor buys across events and comes back — or doesn't — year on year. Some brands here bought in 2025 and 2026; some have a 2027 deal, some have silently churned. Can you surface renewal, net revenue retention, and a churn list the team can act on before the next event?
  2. The account model for multi-brand operators. Our customers are groups trading under many brands/entities/domains. One "customer" can be several companies. When is that parent-child to model, and when is it a duplicate to merge — without losing the detail?
  3. Audience vs. buyer. Most contacts are event attendees, not buyers. How do you build a lifecycle and segmentation that serves both, so the few who buy don't drown in the noise?
  4. Bundled deliverables. A sponsorship deal is a bundle — booth + panel + content + badge (see the line items). How should it be modelled so you can report sold vs. delivered and price/renew well?
  5. Three commercial motions, one CRM. Event sales, media, and rev-share / partnerships behave differently and sit in three inconsistent pipelines. How do you make them coherent enough to forecast?
  6. Lead scoring & routing — unblock the team. Marketing creates leads but the CRM doesn't score them, and routing is manual: most pile on one person while hot inbound sits unassigned for weeks. How would you score leads (fit + intent) and route them automatically, with SLAs, so no one is a bottleneck — and what does HubSpot need set up for marketing to actually run on it?
Tip: state your assumptions — there's no single right answer, and what you'd want that we haven't given you is useful signal too. Don't mass-delete; show us the method.
