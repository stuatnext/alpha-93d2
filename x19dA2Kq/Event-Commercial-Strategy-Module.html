Event module

Event Commercial Strategy Module

A practical module for commercialising conferences, summits, awards, exhibitions, focus events and new event launches.

Sits under the master operating model Applies to events Owner: Commercial Use for annual planning and event cycles

Purpose

This module explains how event projects move from evidence to commercial strategy, GTM, sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, rebooking, delivery and post event review.

It exists because events have specific commercial dependencies: venue choice, agenda quality, speaker access, sponsor inventory, ticket pricing, delivery capacity, floorplan, rebooking, onsite proof and product performance data.

What this module is not

  • It is not a GTM playbook.
  • It is not a marketing calendar.
  • It is not a project delivery plan.
  • It is not a brainstorming document.
  • It is not a replacement for the master operating model.

1. Scope

This module applies to conferences, summits, awards, exhibitions, focus events, retreats, regional events and new event launches.

The master model sets the company sequence. This module explains what that sequence means for events.

The planning depth changes by event size, revenue ambition, strategic importance and risk. The order of decisions should stay consistent.

2. Event planning levels

Event type Planning level What this means
Flagship event Detailed planning Full evidence pack, senior approval, full commercial model, monthly ticket and partnerships pacing, full GTM and full launch readiness check.
Large regional event Detailed or standard planning Products, pricing, sponsor inventory, ticketing, venue, operational assumptions and product performance data confirmed before Sales and Marketing scale.
Focus event Standard planning Sharper buyer focus, leaner inventory, clear sponsor value, product proof and shorter sales and marketing timeline.
Awards Standard planning Category strategy, sponsor category fit, table sales logic, judging integrity, ticket strategy and ceremony delivery clarity.
New event launch Detailed planning Market validation, competitor review, audience proof, category thesis, revenue assumptions and higher scrutiny on risk.
Small tactical event Simplified planning Short evidence pack, clear commercial assumption, named approver, basic CRM tracking and simple launch readiness check.

3. Event operating sequence

0. Confirm event type

Agree size, ambition, risk, timeline and planning level.

1. Event evidence

Collect sales, product, audience, delivery, cost and market evidence.

2. Commercial model

Set products, pricing, packages, inventory, monthly pacing and financial logic.

3. Event GTM

Define positioning, buyer groups, sales direction, marketing direction and handoffs.

4. Setup

Prepare sales, marketing, CRM, collateral, sponsor delivery and event workflows.

5. Launch and review

Sell, market, deliver, report and feed learning into the next cycle.

5. Non negotiables for events

  • Event products should not roll forward because they existed last year.
  • Event product decisions need delivery and engagement data, not only sales opinion. This includes stand traffic, session attendance, scans, WiFi access, website traffic, brand reach, survey data and sponsor interaction data where available.
  • Final collateral should not be built before products, pricing, packages and delivery boundaries are approved.
  • Commercial strategy needs input from Sales, Partnerships, Project Owner, Finance, Conference Content, Marketing, Media, CRM, Event Tech and clients.
  • Rory must be involved where event products affect agenda, speaker value, content quality or sponsor integration.
  • Olivia must sign off the sponsor handoff and delivery model before sponsor fulfilment scales.
  • Project Owners sign off execution dependencies. They do not write the GTM.
  • Monthly partnerships sales targets and monthly ticket sales targets are set during Stage 2.
  • CRM tracking must be ready before Sales and Marketing activity scales.
  • Soft rebooking can start onsite and post event. Formal rebooking needs approved pricing or guardrails.

6. Stage detail

Stage 0: Confirm event type and planning level

1 to 2 working days

Purpose: Agree what kind of event this is, how commercially important it is, how much planning it needs and who needs to approve decisions.

Inputs required

  • Event type and format
  • Event date or target window
  • Venue strategy or known venue constraints
  • Revenue ambition
  • Expected EBITA or profit contribution
  • Expected costs or major cost changes
  • Strategic importance
  • Commercial risk
  • Delivery complexity
  • Timeline pressure
  • Product data availability from the previous cycle, where relevant

Outputs

  • Event type confirmed
  • Planning level agreed
  • Approval owner named
  • Required input list
  • Expected timeline
  • Fast track eligibility
  • Known strategic decisions listed, such as venue move or format change
  • Known product data gaps listed

Checkpoint after Stage 0: Planning route confirmed

Decision owner Decision required Output
Commercial Lead, with Executive Sponsor or Commercial Approver where needed What type of event is this, how much planning does it need, what product data is required and who must approve it? Planning level agreed, required inputs confirmed, owner map clear, product data gaps listed and timeline started.

5 to 10 working days

Purpose: Collect the evidence Commercial needs before products, pricing, packages and GTM are built.

Input Owner Why it matters
What sold, what did not sell, list price versus sold price, discounting and sales objections Sales Lead Shows real market demand, pricing resistance and margin leakage.
Product performance data, including exhibition stand footfall, sponsor scan data, WiFi access, conference session scan-ins, website traffic, brand reach, app engagement, survey feedback and onsite interaction data Project Owner Shows whether each product actually delivered value, not just whether it was sold. This helps decide what should be renewed, repriced, repackaged, upgraded or retired.
Sponsor product proof, including meeting volume, lead capture, badge scans, activation engagement, fulfilment evidence, sponsor feedback and proof capture gaps Partnerships Lead Shows whether sponsor products delivered enough value to renew or justify price increases.
Client and sponsor feedback, churn risk, renewal appetite and upgrade potential Sales Lead Shows what needs protecting, changing or upgrading next cycle.
Sponsor delivery issues, fulfilment risk, proof capture gaps and handoff issues Partnerships Lead Prevents Commercial selling products the company cannot deliver properly.
Event timeline, venue, supplier, staffing, production, registration and onsite constraints Project Owner Defines what the event can realistically support.
Agenda performance, speaker access, content gaps, paid content risk, session attendance, scan-ins and content led product opportunities Conference Content Lead Protects agenda quality and prevents sponsor products damaging event credibility.
Audience mix, buyer quality, delegate behaviour, ticket buyer feedback, website performance and brand reach Marketing Lead Shows whether the event attracted the right people and where conversion, positioning and audience quality can improve.
CRM, registration, ticket source, sponsor enquiry, survey, scan, attendee classification and reporting data CRM Lead Ensures commercial decisions are based on tracked behaviour, not memory or anecdote.
WiFi access, event app engagement, badge scan data, session scan-ins, registration data and onsite technology reporting Event Tech / Registration Owner Provides the behavioural data needed to judge product performance, room quality and sponsor value.
Expected costs, EBITA expectations, margin assumptions, cash timing and payment risks Finance Lead Protects profit and cash before revenue targets are finalised.
Competitor intel, market movement, pricing benchmarks and buyer behaviour changes Commercial Lead Shows how the event should be positioned and priced against the market.

Input completion rule

If required inputs are incomplete after the agreed window, Commercial proceeds using named assumptions. Missing inputs become owned risks. Late inputs do not reopen approved strategy unless Stuart, Ana or Pierre confirm material impact.

Checkpoint after Stage 1: Input readiness

Decision owner Decision required Allowed outcomes
Commercial Lead Are inputs complete enough to build the event commercial model, including the sales data, product performance data and delivery evidence? Proceed. Proceed with assumptions. Pause only the affected decision. Escalate a critical blocker.

11. Escalation logic

Approval window rule

Approval owners have 5 working days to approve, approve with conditions or reject with required changes. If no decision is received, Stuart escalates to Ana or Pierre.

Issue Default action Escalates to
Low risk decision delayed Proceed with documented assumption. Commercial Lead
Pricing, EBITA, margin, cash, monthly sales target or revenue decision delayed Escalate. Do not treat silence as approval. Commercial Approver
Cross team conflict Escalate with options and recommendation. Executive Sponsor
Delivery, sponsor fulfilment, product data or conference content risk Escalate with operational impact and commercial recommendation. Project Owner, Partnerships Lead, Conference Content Lead, Executive Sponsor if unresolved
Fast track route requested Record assumption, risk accepted, decision owner and next checkpoint. Stuart, Ana or Pierre

12. RAG status: Red, Amber, Green

RAG means Red, Amber, Green. It gives Ana and the leadership team a quick view of whether an event is ready, at risk or blocked.

Status Meaning Action
Green Inputs complete, product data available, commercial foundation approved, GTM ready, execution setup on track. Proceed.
Amber Inputs partial, product data incomplete, commercial foundation close, blockers named and owned. Proceed with assumptions or conditional approval.
Red Missing inputs, unapproved pricing, missing EBITA or margin targets, unclear ownership, missing product data or unresolved delivery risk. Escalate and resolve the affected decision.
Blue Fast track route approved. Track risk, owner and next checkpoint.

Partnerships keeps it updated where decisions relate to sponsor delivery or sponsor proof. Conference Content keeps it updated where decisions relate to agenda, speakers or content-led products.

After commercial approval, product, pricing, package, venue, format and sponsor inventory changes require a change request.

15. Meeting rule

  • Pre read required.
  • Brainstorming should happen asynchronously before the meeting.
  • Ideas should be submitted as recommendations with evidence, impact and owner where possible.
  • Meetings approve, reject or assign.
  • No open brainstorming in approval meetings.
  • Every blocker gets an owner and deadline.
  • Every meeting ends with a decision log update.
